# Tennessee — Contractor Compliance Audit
**Last verified:** 2026-05-13
**Sources:** Tennessee Code Annotated (T.C.A.), Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors (TBLC) official rules and publications, Tennessee Department of Revenue, Tennessee Department of Labor & Workforce Development, Tennessee Secretary of State Rules and Public Acts. Secondary sources (law firm briefings, CPA advisories) were used only to locate primary citations; every load-bearing fact below has been verified against the statute or agency document cited.

## 1. Licensing

### 1.1 Licensing authority

The **Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors (TBLC)** sits within the Tennessee Department of Commerce and Insurance and is the sole state agency that issues, renews, suspends, and revokes contractor licenses [1][2].

- **Address:** 500 James Robertson Parkway, Suite 110, Nashville, TN 37243-1150
- **Phone:** (615) 741-8307 / Toll-free (800) 544-7693
- **Fax:** (615) 532-2868
- **Web:** https://www.tn.gov/commerce/regboards/contractor.html

### 1.2 Statutory authority

The licensing scheme is established by **T.C.A. Title 62, Chapter 6** ("Contractors"), with the principal substantive sections at T.C.A. §§ 62-6-101 through 62-6-149 (Part 1, general contractor licensure) and §§ 62-6-501 through 62-6-516 (Part 5, home improvement contractor registration) [1][3]. The Board's own administrative rules are codified at **Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. Chapter 0680-01** (general contractors) and Chapter 0680-07 (home improvement contractors) [4][5].

### 1.3 Project value threshold

Under **T.C.A. § 62-6-103(a)(1)**, any prime contractor undertaking a project where the total cost — labor plus materials — is **$25,000 or more** must hold a TBLC contractor license **before bidding or contracting**. The same threshold applies, per T.C.A. § 62-6-102 (definitions) and § 62-6-103, to subcontractors performing **electrical, mechanical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, or masonry** work when the subcontract value is $25,000 or more, regardless of the prime contract value [3][6].

Performing work that requires a license without holding one is a **Class A misdemeanor** under T.C.A. § 62-6-120, and additionally subjects the contractor to a civil penalty of up to $5,000 and disgorgement of all amounts paid on the unlicensed contract [3][6].

### 1.4 Home improvement contractor (separate registration)

Residential remodeling work between **$3,000 and $24,999** (combined labor and materials) on existing single-family or duplex residences requires a separate **Home Improvement Contractor (HIC)** registration under **T.C.A. §§ 62-6-501 et seq.** [3][7]. As of May 2026 the HIC requirement is enforced in nine Tennessee counties (Bradley, Davidson, Hamilton, Haywood, Knox, Marion, Robertson, Rutherford, and Shelby) plus the cities of Bristol and Johnson City — i.e., it is a county-by-county opt-in rather than a statewide requirement [7].

HIC applicants must post a **$10,000 surety bond** running to the State of Tennessee for the benefit of any aggrieved consumer (T.C.A. § 62-6-506; Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-07-.13) [7][8]. New construction of any size is **not** within the HIC's scope — that requires a full contractor license once the $25,000 threshold is crossed.

### 1.5 License classifications relevant to ContrPro's five trades

The Board's classification system is set out in Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01-.16 (Appendix A to Rule 0680-01-.12) [9] and the Board's current **Classification Outline with Trade Exam Requirements** publication [10]. The classifications and trade exam requirements relevant to ContrPro's five trades are:

| Trade | TBLC Classification | Trade Exam |
|---|---|---|
| General building (commercial/industrial) | **BC** (Building Contractor); subcategories **BC-A** Residential, **BC-A/r** Restricted Residential, **BC-A,b** Residential/Small Commercial, **BC-B** Commercial, **BC-C** Industrial | Required for each subcategory [10] |
| Mechanical (HVAC / plumbing / fire sprinklers / fuel-gas) | **CMC** umbrella with subcategories: **CMC-A** Plumbing & Gas Piping; **CMC-C** HVAC, Refrigeration & Gas Piping; **CMC-D** Sprinklers & Fire Protection; **CMC-E** through **CMC-J** for insulation, pollution control, pneumatic tubes, temperature controls, fuel-gas systems | Required for each [10] |
| Electrical | **CE** with subcategories **CE-A** Substations (with H), **CE-C** Underground Conduit (no exam), **CE-E** Electrical Signs, **CE-F** Telephone Lines & Ducts, **CE-G** Cable TV | Required except CE-C [10] |
| Plumbing | **CMC-A** (Plumbing & Gas Piping), as part of the mechanical classification; statewide plumbing exam requires three (3) years documented plumbing experience [11] | Required [10][11] |
| Structural steel | **S — Erection and Fabrication of Structural Steel**, listed under the Specialty (S) classification in the Board's Classification Outline. Heavy-construction subset is also recognized | Required [10] |

ContrPro should note: a contractor needs a classification for **each** trade it self-performs above the $25,000 threshold. A BC-B prime that self-performs HVAC must independently hold (or sub to) a CMC-C holder.

### 1.6 Experience and examination

Per Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01-.06 and the Board's PSI exam information sheet [12], each applicant (or its qualifying agent) must:

1. Provide documented experience — three (3) years for plumbing classifications [11]; the Board exercises discretion on other classifications based on the nature and dollar size of the requested monetary limit.
2. Pass the **Business and Law Exam** (open-book, ~50 questions, 2-hour limit, 70% passing) using the NASCLA *Contractors Guide to Business, Law and Project Management* (Tennessee edition currently 4th ed.) [12][13].
3. Pass the applicable **trade exam** (BC, CMC-A/C/D, CE, S-Structural Steel, etc.) administered by PSI Services LLC [10][12].

### 1.7 Financial statement requirements by monetary limit (Nov 18 2025 rule change — material)

Tennessee scales the **monetary limit** on a license to **ten (10) times the lesser of working capital and net worth** (Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01-.13) [14][15]. Financial-statement evidence is tiered:

**Initial license applications (no change in 2025-2026):**
- Monetary limit ≤ $3,000,000: **CPA-reviewed** financial statement [14][15].
- Monetary limit > $3,000,000 or "unlimited": **CPA-audited** financial statement (unlimited additionally requires > $300,000 working capital and net worth) [14][15].
- Self-prepared, notarized balance sheet permitted only if applicant requests a monetary limit of **$1,500,000 or less** [14][15].

**Renewals — amended effective November 18, 2025 (Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01, Nov. 2025 Revision):**
The Board amended its renewal-financial-statement rule effective **November 18, 2025**. Under the amended rule, a contractor **renewing** a license with a monetary limit greater than $1,500,000 may now satisfy the financial-statement requirement with a **CPA-compiled** statement, rather than a reviewed or audited statement (which had been the prior renewal standard) [16][17]. The audited-statement requirement is retained at renewal for **unlimited** licenses, and the Board reserves discretion to require a reviewed or audited statement in any case where it determines doing so to be in the public interest [16][17].

Practical effect for ContrPro's customers: a contractor at, e.g., a $5,000,000 monetary limit who in the past paid for a CPA review on every two-year renewal can, beginning with renewals due on or after Nov. 18, 2025, submit a less expensive **compilation** instead. The initial-license tiers are unchanged.

### 1.8 Surety bond / cash deposit (always required for general contractors)

Under T.C.A. § 62-6-108 and Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01-.13 [14][18], every general contractor license issued by the Board must be supported by:

- **$500,000 license bond** if the monetary limit is **less than $1,500,000**, or
- **$1,000,000 license bond** if the monetary limit is **$1,500,000 or greater** [18].

The bond runs in favor of the Board for the benefit of consumers harmed by a violation of Title 62, Chapter 6, and must be issued by a corporate surety authorized to do business in Tennessee.

### 1.9 Insurance minimums

- **Workers' compensation:** Tennessee imposes a **construction-industry-specific** rule under **T.C.A. § 50-6-902** (Part 9, Workers' Compensation Law) [19][20]. Unlike non-construction employers (who are exempt below five employees), every "construction services provider" — including the owner(s) themselves and any uncompensated officers/partners actively engaged in the business — must carry workers' compensation insurance **with one (1) or more employees, including the owner**, unless they file an exemption through the Tennessee Secretary of State's Workers' Compensation Exemption Registry and qualify under the narrow statutory exception [19][20].
- **General liability:** Tennessee statute does **not** specify a minimum general liability limit for contractor licensure. The Board does require attestation of GL coverage as part of the application, and the substantive minimums are driven by individual project owners, public-works specifications, and lenders.

### 1.10 Renewal cycle and continuing education

- **Renewal cycle:** Two (2) years (biennial). Renewal applications are due **30 days before** license expiration (T.C.A. § 62-6-111; Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01-.10) [14][21].
- **Continuing education:** Only **residential** classifications (**BC-A** and **BC-A/r**) carry a mandatory CE requirement — **eight (8) hours** of Board-approved Residential Continuing Education (RCE) every two years, of which up to four (4) hours may be obtained by membership in a Board-approved trade association (Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-09-.02) [22]. Commercial (BC-B), industrial (BC-C), mechanical (CMC), electrical (CE), and specialty (S) licensees have **no statewide CE mandate** as of May 2026.

## 2. Mechanics' lien law (T.C.A. § 66-11)

Tennessee's mechanics' and materialmen's lien is codified at **T.C.A. §§ 66-11-101 through 66-11-208**. Tennessee distinguishes sharply between **prime contractors** (direct contract with the owner) and **remote contractors** (any tier below the prime — subs, sub-subs, suppliers). The two have **very different** notice obligations [23][24][25].

### 2.1 Who can claim a lien

T.C.A. § 66-11-102 grants lien rights to anyone who furnishes labor, materials, equipment, machinery, or professional services (including licensed architects and engineers, and licensed land surveyors) toward the improvement of real property [26].

### 2.2 Prime-contractor lien rights — automatic, but recording is required for priority

A prime contractor's lien attaches automatically by force of statute upon visible commencement of the work (T.C.A. § 66-11-102; § 66-11-104). **No preliminary notice is required** from the prime to preserve the lien against the owner [24]. However, to preserve **priority against subsequent purchasers, mortgagees, and other encumbrancers**, the prime must record a **sworn statement of lien** ("Notice of Lien") with the **register of deeds** in the county where the property is located **within ninety (90) days** after the improvement is complete or abandoned (T.C.A. § 66-11-112) [25][27].

### 2.3 Remote-contractor lien rights — Notice to Owner and Notice of Nonpayment

A remote contractor must satisfy **both** of the following to preserve a lien on **non-residential or 5+ unit residential** projects:

1. **Notice of Nonpayment** under **T.C.A. § 66-11-145**: served by registered or certified mail on both the **owner** and the **prime contractor with whom the remote claimant has contractual privity**, **within ninety (90) days of the last day of each month** in which the remote claimant performed work or supplied materials that remain unpaid [23][28]. The notice must state: (a) the claimant's name and address for communications; (b) a general description of the labor, materials, services, equipment, or machinery provided; (c) the amount owed as of the date of notice; (d) the last date labor or materials were furnished; and (e) a description sufficient to identify the property [28]. **Failure to serve a § 66-11-145 notice for any given month forfeits the lien claim for amounts earned that month**, except as to retainage [28].
2. **Sworn Notice of Lien** recorded with the register of deeds **within ninety (90) days** of the last day the remote claimant furnished labor or materials (T.C.A. § 66-11-115; § 66-11-117) [24][25].

For **one-to-four-family residential** work, T.C.A. § 66-11-146 provides additional remote-claimant requirements (the "Notice to Owner" filing with the register of deeds within the statutory window after the claimant's first work) and recognizes a separate **residential exemption** that limits lien remedies in certain owner-occupied situations [24][25].

### 2.4 Notice to Owner (residential prime contractors) — T.C.A. § 66-11-203

Under the Truth in Construction and Consumer Protection Act of 1975 (T.C.A. §§ 66-11-201 to 66-11-208), a prime contractor about to enter into a contract — written or oral — to improve **residential** real property must deliver a **Notice to Owner** (by registered mail or otherwise) advising the owner of the contractor's lien rights and the one-year duration of those rights [29]. This notice must be given **prior to commencing the work**; a notice delivered after contract execution but before any on-site work is timely [29].

### 2.5 Lien filing deadline summary

| Claimant | Recording window for sworn Notice of Lien | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Prime contractor | 90 days after completion or abandonment | T.C.A. § 66-11-112 [25][27] |
| Remote contractor (commercial / 5+ unit) | 90 days after last work or materials furnished | T.C.A. § 66-11-115; § 66-11-117 [25] |
| Any claimant — Notice of Completion filed | **30 days** (commercial real estate) or **10 days** (residential real estate) after recording of Notice of Completion | T.C.A. § 66-11-143 [27][30] |

### 2.6 Notice of Completion (T.C.A. § 66-11-143) — accelerator clause

When the owner (or its agent) records a **Notice of Completion** with the register of deeds and serves it on all known potential lien claimants, the statutory filing window collapses from 90 days to **30 days (commercial)** or **10 days (residential)** from the date the Notice of Completion is recorded [27][30]. A working CFO should treat receipt of any owner-served Notice of Completion as a hard deadline.

### 2.7 Lien duration and enforcement

Per **T.C.A. § 66-11-106** and § 66-11-115, the lien remains effective for **one (1) year** after the date the improvement is completed or abandoned (or, for a remote claimant, one year after the date of recording the Notice of Lien). **Suit to enforce the lien must be commenced within that one-year window** [31][32]. If the owner serves a **written demand to enforce**, the claimant has only **sixty (60) days from service** to file suit (T.C.A. § 66-11-130) [31][32]. Missing either deadline extinguishes the lien.

### 2.8 Sworn statement of contract on owner demand (T.C.A. § 66-11-145(c))

Section 66-11-145 separately gives the owner the right to demand from any prime or remote contractor a **sworn statement of all unpaid amounts owed to that contractor and to its lower-tier claimants**. Failure of a prime contractor to provide the sworn statement, when demanded, may bar enforcement of the prime's lien [28].

### 2.9 Bond to indemnify (T.C.A. § 66-11-142) — releasing a recorded lien

If a lien has been recorded, **any interested person** (typically the owner, lender, or prime contractor) may discharge the property from the lien by recording with the register of deeds a **bond to indemnify** against the lien, equal to the lien amount, with sufficient corporate surety authorized to do business in Tennessee, conditioned on satisfying any judgment in favor of the lien claimant [33]. Recording the bond automatically operates as a release of the lien (T.C.A. § 66-11-142(c)); the lien claimant retains its action against the obligors on the bond. A payment bond previously furnished by the prime contractor under T.C.A. § 66-11-124 may be recorded in lieu of a fresh discharge bond [33].

## 3. Prompt payment and retainage (T.C.A. § 66-34 — Tennessee Prompt Pay Act)

The Tennessee Prompt Pay Act, T.C.A. §§ 66-34-101 through 66-34-704, governs payment timing and retainage on **both public and private** construction contracts (with limited exclusions). The Tennessee Supreme Court has confirmed the Act applies broadly and provides a private right of action [34][35].

### 3.1 Owner-to-prime payment timing — T.C.A. § 66-34-202

The owner (or its agent) must pay the prime contractor **in accordance with the schedule established in the contract**, but in no event later than **thirty (30) days after the prime contractor's timely application for payment** [36]. Wrongfully withheld amounts accrue **interest from the date due** at the contract rate, or, if no rate is specified, at **1.5% per month** (T.C.A. § 66-34-601). Interest unpaid for 60 days is capitalized into the principal and continues to compound [36][37].

### 3.2 Prime-to-subcontractor payment timing — T.C.A. § 66-34-302

The prime must pay each subcontractor (remote contractor) in accordance with the subcontract, but **no later than the payment timing set in the subcontract** following receipt of payment from the owner. Interest accrues on wrongfully withheld payments at the same statutory rate [38].

### 3.3 Retainage cap — T.C.A. § 66-34-103

**Maximum retainage:** **5%** of the contract amount, on both public and private contracts. Any provision in a construction contract purporting to authorize retainage in excess of 5% is **void as against public policy** (T.C.A. § 66-34-103(a)) [39][40].

### 3.4 Mandatory third-party escrow for retainage — T.C.A. § 66-34-104

For any prime contract **with a value of $500,000 or more**, retainage withheld by the owner **must be deposited into a separate, interest-bearing escrow account with a third-party escrow agent** at the time of withholding (T.C.A. § 66-34-104) [39][41]. The Act expressly excludes (a) home improvement contracts, and (b) construction of one to four single-family units [41].

### 3.5 Penalty for failure to escrow — T.C.A. § 66-34-104(c)

A party that fails to deposit withheld retainage into the required third-party escrow account is liable for damages of **$300 per day** for every day the funds are not properly escrowed [39][41]. Additionally, failure to deposit retainage into escrow **within seven (7) days of receipt of written notice of the failure** is a **Class A misdemeanor** [39][41].

### 3.6 Retainage release timing — T.C.A. § 66-34-103(b)

The owner must release and pay all retainage to the prime contractor **within ninety (90) days after completion of the work or within ninety (90) days after substantial completion of the project, whichever occurs first** [39][42]. The prime must then pay each remote contractor's portion of retainage **within ten (10) days of receiving it from the owner**, and each remote contractor must pay its lower-tier remote contractors **within ten (10) days of receipt** [39][42].

### 3.7 Right to suspend work — "reasonable assurances" (T.C.A. § 66-34-602)

If an owner fails to make payment when due, the prime contractor may, **after giving the owner ten (10) days' written notice** and a reasonable opportunity to cure, **suspend further performance** until paid. If the owner fails to furnish "reasonable assurances" of future payment after written request, the contractor likewise has the right to suspend (T.C.A. § 66-34-602) [43].

### 3.8 Public vs. private — material differences

The Prompt Pay Act applies to both. Specific differences:

- The escrow requirement applies only when the **prime contract value is $500,000 or more** (T.C.A. § 66-34-104) [41].
- The Act does **not** apply to residential construction of one to four single-family units, nor to home improvement contracts as defined in Title 62, Chapter 6, Part 5 [41].
- On **public works contracts**, additional payment-bond requirements under T.C.A. § 12-4-201 et seq. ("Little Miller Act") supplement the Prompt Pay Act and are not waived by it.

## 4. Tax structure for contractors

### 4.1 State and local sales/use tax rates

- **State rate:** **7.0%** on most tangible personal property, plus a separate **2.75%** state-level "single article" rate that applies only to the portion of a single item's sales price between $1,600 and $3,200 (T.C.A. § 67-6-202) [44][45].
- **Local option rate:** Local jurisdictions may levy up to **2.75%** in 0.25% increments under T.C.A. § 67-6-702 [45][46]. As of May 2026, **all 95 counties** levy some local option rate. The maximum combined state + local rate is therefore **9.75%**, in effect in Memphis, Nashville's nearer suburbs, Murfreesboro, Kingsport, Cleveland, Johnson City, and many others [46].

### 4.2 Contractor sales-tax treatment — contractor is the consumer

Tennessee follows the majority rule: when a contractor improves real property, the **contractor is treated as the final consumer of the materials it incorporates**, and **pays sales/use tax at the point of purchase** on those materials [47][48]. Key consequences:

- The contractor does **not** collect sales tax from the project owner on either materials or labor in a real-property-improvement contract.
- Materials purchased out-of-state and brought into Tennessee for incorporation into real property are subject to **use tax** at the same combined state + local rate (T.C.A. § 67-6-203) [47].
- **Separately stated labor** for repair services to real property is generally **not** subject to sales tax. (Repair labor on **tangible personal property** is taxable — a different rule that occasionally trips up mixed contractors.)
- Specific tangible-personal-property installations (e.g., a free-standing piece of equipment not affixed to realty) are **taxable retail sales**, and the contractor must collect sales tax from the customer on the entire charge.

The governing statute is **T.C.A. § 67-6-541** (sales to or use by a contractor, subcontractor, or material vendor), supplemented by Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 1320-05-01 and the Tennessee Department of Revenue's Important Notice **SUT-21** (Sales and Use Tax for Contractors — Overview) [47][48][49].

### 4.3 Real property improvement vs. tangible personal property installation

The line is functional, not contractual. The Department of Revenue applies the common-law fixture test: degree of physical annexation, adaptation to the realty, and intent. HVAC equipment ducted into the building, plumbing rough-in, and structural steel embedded into the building shell are real-property improvements (contractor consumes/pays use tax on materials). Modular machinery installed on a slab and bolted down, with all connections being utility hook-ups, may be tangible personal property (contractor charges sales tax to customer) — fact-specific [47][49].

### 4.4 Business tax (gross-receipts) — T.C.A. § 67-4-708 et seq.

Contractors are subject to Tennessee **Business Tax** under T.C.A. Title 67, Chapter 4, Part 7. Most general contractors fall in **Classification 4** (contractors), taxed at **0.001875** (0.1875%) of gross receipts from Tennessee jobs annually [50]. Business tax is administered by the Department of Revenue but a county/city business license is also required. Contractors whose **total Tennessee receipts at a single location are $100,000 or more** must register and pay state business tax; receipts below that threshold are reported only at the local level.

### 4.5 Franchise and excise tax — T.C.A. §§ 67-4-2007 and 67-4-2104

Any contractor organized as a corporation, LLC, limited partnership, or business trust (or doing business in Tennessee as such an entity) is subject to:

- **Franchise tax:** **0.25%** of the greater of (a) net worth or (b) the actual value of real and tangible property owned or used in Tennessee, with a **$100 minimum** (T.C.A. § 67-4-2105) [51].
- **Excise tax:** **6.5%** of Tennessee taxable net earnings (T.C.A. § 67-4-2007) [51].

Sole proprietorships and general partnerships are **not** subject to franchise & excise tax. Single-member LLCs are generally **not** disregarded for Tennessee F&E purposes (a key trap) — they file their own F&E return — except for SMLLCs whose single member is a corporation [51][52].

## 5. Recent changes (2024–2026)

### 5.1 November 18, 2025 — TBLC rule amendment, renewal financial statements

The Board adopted an amendment to **Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. 0680-01** effective **November 18, 2025** that permits a **CPA-compiled** financial statement (rather than reviewed or audited) on **renewal** for licenses with monetary limits exceeding $1,500,000. Unlimited-license renewals still require an audited statement, and initial-license tiers are unchanged. The legislative origin traces to HB1903 of the 113th General Assembly (introduced 2023–24); the policy change was ultimately implemented through Board rulemaking rather than statute [16][17][53].

### 5.2 Construction Industry Payment Protection Act ("CIPPA") — 2020 baseline, still operative

The CIPPA package (Public Chapter 605 of 2020; SB2681/HB2706) modernized Tennessee's Prompt Pay Act and Title 66 lien provisions and remains the operative framework as of May 2026 [54]. No subsequent General Assembly has materially amended its core retainage cap, escrow threshold, or § 66-11-145 notice-of-nonpayment mechanics.

### 5.3 114th General Assembly (2025–2026) — search results

A targeted review of the 114th General Assembly bill tracker (legiscan.com and capitol.tn.gov for Title 62 and Title 66 bills) as of May 13, 2026 identifies **no enacted bill** that materially changes the licensing thresholds, lien deadlines, prompt-pay timing, or retainage cap described in this audit. **HB2530** (and its Senate companion) addresses allocation of TBLC license-fee revenues with respect to the Go Build Tennessee Act; it does not alter substantive licensing or lien rules [55]. **HB6001** (114th G.A.) and several smaller bills touching Title 66 were introduced but had not been enacted as of the verification date [55].

### 5.4 Discrepancy resolution

One secondary source, paraphrasing **HB0271 of the 111th General Assembly (2019)**, reported amendments extending the § 66-11-145 nonpayment notice window from 90 days to 12 months. **That bill was not enacted in that form**; the current statutory language at T.C.A. § 66-11-145(a) retains the **90-day** monthly cadence (verified against the 2024 Tennessee Code as published on law.justia.com) [23][28]. ContrPro should treat **90 days from the last day of each month** as the controlling deadline.

## 6. Practical compliance checklist for a Tennessee contractor

**Before the bid is signed:**
- Confirm the project value, labor + materials. If **≥ $25,000**, the prime and any electrical/mechanical/plumbing/HVAC/roofing/masonry sub at $25,000+ must hold a current TBLC license **before bidding** (T.C.A. § 62-6-103).
- Confirm the appropriate classification is on the license (BC-A/B/C, CMC-A/C/D, CE, S-Structural Steel, etc.).
- Confirm the monetary limit on the license covers the bid amount; if not, file an Increase Monetary Limit application supported by the appropriate financial statement tier.

**Before commencing residential work:**
- Deliver the **Notice to Owner** required by T.C.A. § 66-11-203 (registered mail or equivalent).

**During the work (every month, every project, every claimant tier below prime):**
- If you are a **remote contractor** and have unpaid amounts, **serve a § 66-11-145 Notice of Nonpayment** by certified mail on the owner and prime contractor **within 90 days of the last day of the month** the unpaid work was performed. Calendar it monthly.

**During the work (escrow):**
- If the prime contract is **≥ $500,000**, confirm the **owner has deposited retainage in a third-party, interest-bearing escrow account** at the time of withholding. Document the deposit. **$300/day** damages apply if it is not escrowed.

**On owner non-payment:**
- Pay-when-paid windows: owner-to-prime within **30 days** of timely application; prime-to-sub per subcontract.
- If owner is late: give **10 days' written notice** under § 66-34-602 and the right to suspend work attaches; interest accrues at the contract rate or **1.5%/month** statutory default.

**At project closeout:**
- **Owner must release retainage within 90 days** of completion or substantial completion. **Prime must pass through within 10 days** of receipt; remote contractors must do the same.

**Within 90 days of last work or abandonment (lien preservation):**
- File a sworn **Notice of Lien** with the **register of deeds** in the county of the property (T.C.A. § 66-11-112 / -115).
- **If the owner records a Notice of Completion**, the window collapses to **30 days (commercial) or 10 days (residential)** — treat receipt of that notice as a hard deadline.

**Within 1 year of last work / abandonment (lien enforcement):**
- File suit to enforce the lien (T.C.A. § 66-11-106). **If the owner serves a written demand to enforce, the window collapses to 60 days** from service of demand.

**Annual / biennial compliance:**
- File and pay **business tax** (gross receipts) on Classification 4 schedule.
- File and pay **franchise & excise tax** annually (corporations, LLCs, LPs).
- Pay **sales/use tax** on materials purchases throughout the year; file consumer use tax returns for out-of-state materials.
- **Renew the contractor license** at least **30 days before expiration**. For BC-A/BC-A/r, complete **8 hours of RCE** before submitting renewal. If renewing above $1.5M monetary limit, submit a CPA-compiled statement (post Nov-18-2025) or audited statement for unlimited.
- Maintain workers' compensation coverage **on all employees including owners** (construction-industry rule, T.C.A. § 50-6-902), or file a valid exemption with the Secretary of State's WC Exemption Registry.
- Maintain the **$500K or $1M license bond** (depending on monetary limit) — renew the surety annually.
