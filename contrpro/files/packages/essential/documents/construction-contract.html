6.4 Pay-When-Paid vs. Pay-If-Paid

Before Using This Contract:

  1. Have an attorney review — This is a template, not legal advice. Have a construction attorney licensed in the Project state review and customize every clause, especially indemnification, dispute resolution, venue, lien waivers, and any consumer / home-improvement provisions.
  2. Confirm the pricing method — Only one of Sections 3.2, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, or 3.6 should govern; delete or strike inapplicable sections to avoid ambiguity.
  3. Build the Schedule of Values — Complete Exhibit B before signing. Front-loaded SOVs invite disputes.
  4. Resolve state-specific issues — Anti-indemnity statutes, pay-if-paid prohibitions, venue restrictions, statutes of repose, prompt-payment timelines, and prevailing-wage applicability all vary by state. Apply Exhibit J.
  5. Attach all exhibits — Drawings, specifications, addenda, insurance certificates and endorsements, lien waiver forms, schedules, subcontractor list, bonds, BIM/safety plans.
  6. Verify licensing — Confirm Contractor's license number, classification, and good standing in the Project state. Many states render contracts unenforceable when the contractor is unlicensed.
  7. Verify insurance — Certificates and endorsements should be reviewed by Owner's risk-management or broker for compliance with Article 8 BEFORE the Effective Date.
  8. Confirm prevailing-wage / E-Verify / DBE obligations — If the Project is public, federally funded, or in a state requiring E-Verify, verify enrollment and posting compliance.
  9. Confirm consumer / home-improvement compliance — Residential work in states like California, New York, Massachusetts, and others requires statutory contract terms, font sizes, mandatory disclosures, and a right of rescission. Apply Exhibit J as needed.
  10. Coordinate with lender — If the Project is financed, the lender may require additional protections (consent rights, assignment of contract, payment certifications, change-order approvals).
  11. Coordinate with the bond producer — If bonded, the bond forms and Surety acknowledgment must be in place at execution.
  12. Confirm jurisdictional venue and choice-of-law — Out-of-state forum / law clauses are void in many states for in-state construction; default to Project state.
  13. Check local ordinances — Some municipalities require additional contract language, registration, or permits beyond state law.
  14. Confirm tax treatment — Sales / use / gross-receipts tax allocation varies by state and project type; complete Section 3.9.
  15. Sign in counterparts with electronic signatures only after review — Once signed, this Agreement and its Exhibits become the comprehensive controlling instrument.
