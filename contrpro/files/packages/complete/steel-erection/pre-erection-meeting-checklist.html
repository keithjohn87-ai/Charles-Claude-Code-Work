USE BEFORE THE FIRST STICK OF STEEL LEAVES THE SHOP

The Pre-Erection Meeting ("PEM" or "MEC" — Multi-Employer Coordination) is the single most important coordination event in a steel-erection project. AISC Code of Standard Practice §4.3 and 29 CFR 1926.752 both contemplate a documented pre-erection meeting between the steel erector, the controlling contractor, the EOR, the fabricator, and adjacent-trade subs. Use this checklist as both the meeting agenda AND the meeting minutes. Sign at the end. File a signed copy in the project record and post a copy at the jobsite trailer. Most steel-erection disputes — change-order rejections, schedule disputes, safety citations — can be traced to a meeting item that was skipped or never documented.

Two purposes, one document. Section 1 is the attendance roster — who's required, who shows up, what each one signs into. Sections 2-14 are the meeting agenda items; each section is structured so the meeting chair walks through it, the discussion captures key decisions in the lines provided, and the action items at the end of each section get assigned. Section 15 is the signature block — every required attendee signs to confirm receipt of the items discussed. The signed document is the project's evidentiary record of the coordination meeting.

Section 7 — Fall Protection Responsibility Matrix

Per OSHA Multi-Employer Citation Policy, fall-protection responsibility must be clearly assigned. The matrix below is reviewed and accepted by the controlling contractor, the steel erector, and each downstream sub at this meeting.

System / LocationInstallerMaintainer During Steel ErectionHandover TriggerMaintainer After Handover
PFAS anchor points on steelErector (engineered)ErectorSubstantial completion of steelErector / GC (jointly until demob)
Perimeter cable / guardrail (open edges)[Erector / GC][Erector / GC][Bay handover][GC]
Floor-hole covers + skylight protection[GC / Trade installing the opening]GCHole createdGC throughout
Stairs & landings (during install)ErectorErectorFinal attachment + handrailGC
Internal floor edges (atrium, openings)[Erector / GC]GCDecking completeGC
Wall openings (curtainwall reveals)GCGCN/AGC
Document the handover, every time. Bay-by-bay handover from erector to GC for ongoing fall-protection responsibility should be documented on a separate Bay Handover Log (signed by both PMs). Without that paper, a fall in a bay weeks after erection becomes a multi-citation event with no clear single responsibility. The PEM is where you establish the principle; the Bay Handover Log is where you operationalize it.

Closing reminder. The PEM is not a formality — it is the primary mechanism by which the OSHA Multi-Employer Citation Policy is navigated and by which AISC §4.3 coordination obligations are documented. A skipped or undocumented PEM transfers risk to the erector and creates exposure for the controlling contractor. Hold the meeting; sign the document; file the original; revise this checklist when project conditions change materially.
