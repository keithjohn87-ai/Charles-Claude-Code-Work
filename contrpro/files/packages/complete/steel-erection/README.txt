ContrPro Steel Erection Trade Pack — Complete Tier
====================================================

Trade-specific delta on top of the Universal Sub Suite for structural steel
erectors. Field-only scope (fabrication shop work is out of scope).

USE THIS WITH THE UNIVERSAL SUB SUITE.
This pack does NOT replicate the universal forms (Bid Package, Subcontract
Review, Sub SOV, T&M Tracker, Backcharge Dispute, Certified Payroll, Joint
Check, Daily Field Report). Those live in ../sub/ and apply to every trade
identically. This pack adds the trade-specific tools an erector needs ON TOP
of the universal forms.

====================================================
What you get — 6 deliverables, 8 files
====================================================

1. Steel Erection Bid Estimator
   (Steel_Erection_Bid_Estimator.xlsx)
   11-tab tonnage-based bid estimator. CSI Division 05 primary with cross-refs
   to 03 (concrete embeds), 07 (fireproofing), 09 (paint).
     - Instructions, Project Info, Wage Rates (ironworker classifications +
       fringe burden + OT premium), Equipment Rates (crane + support),
       Material Takeoff (CSI-coded line items), Labor Estimate (hrs/UOM ×
       crew × rate with productivity defaults), Equipment Estimate,
       Subcontract Estimate (bolting/welding/fireproofing if subbed),
       Premiums & Conditions (height/AESS/distance/complexity adders),
       Bid Summary (roll-up by CSI division → contingency, bond, OH, profit),
       CSI Reference.

2. Site-Specific Erection Plan
   (site-specific-erection-plan.html / .docx)
   16 sections. Federally-required document per 29 CFR 1926.752(c) covering
   the entire Subpart R compliance map: controlling-contractor §1926.752(a)
   notification, site layout/sequence, equipment, hoisting, anchorage, beam
   & column connection criteria, joists, decking, falling objects, fall
   protection, training, emergency response, multi-employer coordination,
   special procedures, signatures.

3. Pre-Erection Meeting Checklist
   (pre-erection-meeting-checklist.html / .docx)
   15 sections — agenda + minutes for the AISC §4.3 / OSHA §1926.752 multi-
   employer coordination meeting. 15-row attendance roster, §1926.752(a)
   notification verification, SSEP review, crane qualifications + critical
   lifts, fall-protection responsibility matrix with bay-handover doctrine,
   submittals, insurance, emergency response, weather stop-work thresholds,
   Day-1 first-lift plan, action items, signatures.

4. Crane & Rigging Lift Plan + Critical Lift Calculator
   (crane-and-rigging-lift-plan.html / .docx + Critical_Lift_Calculator.xlsx)
   11-section procedural document + 8-tab math workbook.
     HTML: Roles (1926.1427-1432), Equipment & Inspections (1926.1412),
     Rigging Standards (ASME B30.9), Standard Lift Procedure, Critical Lift
     Criteria + Required Steps, Tandem Lift, Personnel Platform (1926.1431),
     Energized Lines Table A (1926.1408), Ground Conditions (1926.1402),
     Stop-Work Triggers, printable Critical Lift Permit.
     XLSX: Pick Weight, Crane Capacity Check (load chart input + critical-
     lift trigger), Sling Angle Check (per-sling load with angle de-rate),
     Ground Bearing Pressure, Tandem Lift, printable Permit Summary.

5. Bolt Installation & Inspection Guide + Bolt Inspection Log
   (bolt-installation-and-inspection-guide.html / .docx +
    Bolt_Inspection_Log.xlsx)
   10-section RCSC-spec guide + 7-tab daily inspection workbook.
     HTML: ASTM F3125 / F3148 grades, three connection types (snug-tight /
     pretensioned / slip-critical), four pretensioning methods (TOTN with
     RCSC Table 8.2 rotations, calibrated wrench, twist-off, DTI), Class A/B/C
     faying surfaces, Skidmore-Wilhelm pre-installation verification, routine
     observation, sampling, mill-cert chain, 10 common errors.
     XLSX: Bolt Lot Master (with RCSC Table 8.1 pretension auto-lookup),
     Pre-Installation Verification Log (Skidmore tests), Connection Log
     (per-connection install + inspection), Daily Roll-Up.

6. Plumb-and-True Tolerance Guide + Tolerance Survey Log
   (plumb-and-true-tolerance-guide.html / .docx +
    Tolerance_Survey_Log.xlsx)
   11-section AISC §7.13 compliance guide + 8-tab survey workbook.
     HTML: §7.13 framework, erector/fab/owner responsibilities, anchor rod
     tolerances, column plumb 1:500 with column-length reference table,
     member straightness, floor levelness, building drift, survey methods,
     survey cadence, out-of-tolerance RFI protocol, as-built sign-off.
     XLSX: Anchor Rod Survey (theoretical vs as-found with radial deviation),
     Column Plumb Log (X+Y offset with auto-flag against project plumb ratio),
     Floor Elevation Log, Deviation/RFI Tracker, Daily Summary.

====================================================
CSI MasterFormat Integration
====================================================

Every back-office tool in this pack is CSI-coded. The Bid Estimator uses
CSI Division 05 (Metals) as primary, with cross-references to:
  - 03 30 00  Cast-in-Place Concrete (embeds supplied by erector, set by concrete sub)
  - 05 12 23  Structural Steel Framing
  - 05 12 33  Architecturally Exposed Structural Steel (AESS)
  - 05 21 19  Open-Web Steel Joists
  - 05 21 23  Joist Girders
  - 05 31 13  Steel Floor Decking
  - 05 31 23  Steel Roof Decking
  - 05 50 00  Metal Fabrications
  - 05 51 13  Metal Pan Stairs
  - 05 52 13  Pipe and Tube Railings
  - 07 81 00  Applied Fireproofing (cross-ref — typically separate sub)
  - 09 91 00  Painting / Touch-Up (cross-ref)

The Bid Summary tab on Steel_Erection_Bid_Estimator.xlsx rolls up totals by
CSI division so the bid integrates cleanly into the GC's job-cost accounting
AND into your monthly billing via the Universal Sub Suite's
Sub_Schedule_of_Values.xlsx.

====================================================
File formats — your tools, our forms
====================================================

Every document ships in multiple formats:
  - .html — source of truth, open in any browser, print to PDF
  - .docx — Word; opens in Microsoft Word, Apple Pages, LibreOffice,
            and uploads as-is to Google Docs (File → Open → upload)
  - .xlsx — Excel workbooks; open in Excel, Numbers, Google Sheets,
            LibreOffice Calc

====================================================
Field-only scope
====================================================

This trade pack is calibrated for field erection only. Fabrication shop work
(cutting, drilling, welding, painting, galvanizing, AESS shop finishes) is
out of scope and would require a separate fabrication-shop estimator with
different rate structures, productivity, and quality systems.

====================================================
Important reminders
====================================================

These templates are tailored to common commercial-construction practice and
parallel federal regulations (29 CFR 1926 Subpart R and Subpart CC), the AISC
Code of Standard Practice, the RCSC Specification, and ASTM standards
without reproducing copyrighted material. They are NOT legal advice and they
are NOT a substitute for the erector's Competent Person, the controlling
contractor's safety oversight, the EOR's structural review, or a third-party
Special Inspector's program.

Before first use on any new project, have construction counsel and an OSHA-
experienced safety consultant review the tailored documents. Federal
regulations are current as of January 2026 — verify against the latest CFR,
AISC, RCSC, and ASTM publications.
