FEDERALLY REQUIRED — TAILOR + SIGN BEFORE FIRST STEEL ARRIVES

29 CFR 1926.752(e) requires that, before steel erection begins, the controlling contractor provide written notification to the steel erector confirming that (1) the concrete in the footings, piers, and walls and the mortar in masonry piers and walls has attained 75% of the minimum specified compressive strength, and (2) any repairs/modifications/replacements of anchor rods have been corrected. 29 CFR 1926.752(c) requires every commercial steel erector to develop and maintain a Site-Specific Erection Plan when so directed by the controlling contractor or when the erector elects to use one. This is the template for that plan. Tailor every variable to the actual project, conduct the pre-erection meeting, sign at Section 16, and post a copy at the jobsite trailer. The OSHA Multi-Employer Citation Policy applies — the controlling contractor, the steel erector, and the lower-tier subs are all citable for site-specific violations regardless of who created them.

Section 5 — Hoisting & Rigging Procedures
5.1 — Rigging Inspection (29 CFR 1926.251)

All rigging — slings, shackles, hooks, taglines, spreader bars — shall be inspected before each shift by the rigger or signal person. Damaged or out-of-tolerance components are removed from service. Documentation kept on file. Sling inspection criteria per ASME B30.9.

5.2 — Standard Lift Procedure
  1. Site Superintendent / Foreman confirms lift plan and rigging configuration before each lift.
  2. Lift Director (1926.1432) reviews crane load chart and confirms pick weight is within capacity at the lift radius.
  3. Tag lines attached to all loads to control rotation/swing — minimum two tag lines for any piece >2,000 lb.
  4. Signal Person and Operator confirm hand-signal or radio communication channel. Radio is the default on signal-blocked sites.
  5. Exclusion zone established — no personnel below suspended loads (1926.759(a)). Connectors and bolt-up crews maintain 360° awareness.
  6. Operator pre-lift checks: outriggers/tracks set, level, ground bearing adequate, swing path clear, energized-line clearance verified (1926.1408).
  7. Lift executed — load lifted just clear of supports to verify rigging, then proceeds at controlled speed.
  8. Load placed and seated — connectors install minimum-required bolts before load is released from rigging (see Section 7).
  9. Tag lines removed AFTER bolts are in.
5.3 — Critical Lifts

A critical lift is defined per 29 CFR 1926.1432 and the controlling contractor's policy. Indicators include: (a) load weight ≥75% of crane capacity at the lift radius, (b) multi-crane lift, (c) lift over occupied structure, (d) personnel suspension (1926.1431). Critical lifts require a separate written Critical Lift Plan (use Critical_Lift_Calculator.xlsx) with the EOR's review (when over occupied structure) and a pre-lift huddle with all participants.

Tandem lifts. Two-crane lifts have an outsized failure rate when not pre-planned. Tandem lifts require: matched-speed operators with simultaneous training, identical load charts for the lift configuration, written sequence describing pick-and-place choreography, a single Lift Director with override authority, and an EOR review of the rigging geometry. Do not undertake a tandem lift on field improvisation.

Section 7 — Beam & Column Connection Criteria (29 CFR 1926.756)
29 CFR 1926.756(a)(1) — During the final placing of solid web structural members, the load shall not be released from the hoisting line until the members are secured with at least two bolts per connection, of the same size and strength as shown in the erection drawings, drawn up wrench-tight or the equivalent as specified by the project structural engineer of record. 1926.756(b) — A competent person shall determine if more than two bolts are necessary to ensure the stability of cantilevered members; if additional bolts are needed, they shall be installed.
7.1 — Minimum Bolt Requirements
  • Solid-web members: ≥2 bolts per connection, drawn up wrench-tight, before load release.
  • Cantilevered solid-web members: Competent Person evaluates need for >2 bolts; install as needed.
  • Diagonal bracing connections: Per project structural-erection drawings — typically all bolts in before release.
  • Column splices: Per drawings — typically all bolts in before release.
  • Open-web steel joists: See Section 8 (1926.757 governs).
7.2 — Plumbing-Up Procedure
  1. Columns plumbed and braced after every two-bay placement, before the next bay's beams set.
  2. Plumbing accomplished by adjustable diagonal-bracing cables / temporary bracing, controlled at base by ironworkers.
  3. Final plumb-and-true survey performed bay-by-bay using total station; deviations recorded on Tolerance Survey Log (see Tolerance_Survey_Log.xlsx).
  4. Permanent bolting at column splices completed only after the column is within AISC Code of Standard Practice §7.13 tolerances.
7.3 — Wrench-Tight Standard

"Wrench-tight" per RCSC Specification = the full effort of an ironworker on an ordinary spud wrench, bringing the connected parts into firm contact. NOT pretensioned. Pretensioned bolting (turn-of-nut, calibrated wrench, twist-off, DTI) follows separately per the Bolt Installation & Inspection Log workflow.

Section 8 — Open-Web Steel Joists (29 CFR 1926.757)
8.1 — Joist Stabilization

OSHA 1926.757 contains detailed and prescriptive requirements for joist stabilization that differ from solid-web members. Highlights:

  • Hoisting cables shall not be released from joists with a bottom-chord bearing seat until joist has been field-bolted at both ends (1926.757(a)(1)).
  • For joists 40 ft (12.2 m) or longer, all bridging shall be installed and anchored, the bolts/bridging connections shall be tight, and ALL columns and bridging anchored before the hoisting cables are released (1926.757(a)(2)).
  • A row of bolted diagonal erection bridging shall be required at the ends of joists 40 ft or longer (1926.757(a)(3)).
  • Field-bolted joists shall not be loaded with construction loads until they are fully bolted and bridged in accordance with the erection drawings (1926.757(c)(2)).
  • No employee shall be permitted on any steel joist where (a) the joist is over 40 ft long AND has not been fully bridged, OR (b) loading is unsafe per the erection drawings (1926.757(c)(3)).

Section 10 — Falling Object Protection (29 CFR 1926.759)
  • Securing loose items: All materials, equipment, and tools not in immediate use shall be secured against accidental displacement. Bolt bags zipped or tied. Tool tethers required for ALL tools used above grade.
  • Overhead loads: No employee in or below a CDZ or directly below a load other than employees actively engaged in the connecting or installing work. Exclusion zone radius set by Lift Director.
  • Riveting tools, bolts, fasteners: Stored in containers with secure closures.
  • Below-grade staging: Toe boards / debris netting on edges where personnel work below.
Section 11 — Fall Protection (29 CFR 1926.760)
29 CFR 1926.760(a)(1) — Except as provided by paragraph (a)(3) of this section, each employee engaged in a steel erection activity who is on a walking/working surface with an unprotected side or edge more than 15 feet (4.6 m) above a lower level shall be protected from fall hazards by guardrail systems, safety net systems, personal fall arrest systems, positioning device systems, or fall restraint systems.
11.1 — Fall Protection Trigger

Steel erection fall protection threshold is 15 feet (different from the 6-foot general construction trigger of §1926.501). Below 15 feet, fall protection is not required by §1926.760, though guardrails on completed floors and decking are required per other sections (e.g., §1926.501 for non-steel-erection work).

Creating EmployerEmployer who creates the hazard.Steel erector creates the fall hazard at heights >15 ft.
Exposing EmployerEmployer whose own employees are exposed.Erector + any sub working concurrently in the erection area.
Correcting EmployerEmployer responsible for correcting hazards.Erector for steel-erection hazards; GC for site-wide.

  • Lower-tier sub access to bays after erection (MEP, decking, fireproofing).
  • Crane move days and adjacent-trade exclusion.
  • Site-wide fall-protection responsibility (perimeter cable, hole covers).
