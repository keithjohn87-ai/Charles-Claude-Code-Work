Section 3 — Rigging Standards
29 CFR 1926.251 — Rigging equipment for material handling; ASME B30.9 — Slings; ASME B30.10 — Hooks; ASME B30.20 — Below-the-hook lifting devices; ASME B30.26 — Rigging hardware.
3.1 — Sling Selection & Capacity
  • Sling rated capacity at the actual hitch (vertical / choker / basket) must equal or exceed the share of the load on that sling, with appropriate de-rating for sling angle.
  • Sling angle below 60° from horizontal de-rates capacity sharply — sling load = (load × share) ÷ sin(angle). At 30° the sling sees 2× the vertical share.
  • Choker hitch capacity is 75% of vertical for synthetic; 80% for wire rope.
  • Wire-rope clips installed per 1926.251(c)(5) Table H-20 — number, spacing, torque.
  • Synthetic slings inspected for cuts, snags, melted fibers, broken stitching, missing tag, sun damage.
  • Sling tags must be legible and intact. Untagged slings are removed from service.
3.2 — Hardware
  • Shackles: bolt or screw-pin, rated capacity stamped, no field substitutions, no painted-over capacity stamps.
  • Hooks: rated capacity, equipped with safety latch (closed when in use), no spreading or wear >10% of original section.
  • Eyebolts: shoulder type only for angular loads; torqued to manufacturer spec; thread engagement minimum 1× diameter.
  • Spreader bars: rated and certified; rigging hooks at correct geometry for the pick.
3.3 — Tag Lines

Minimum two tag lines for any load > 2,000 lb. Tag lines installed and tested before the load leaves the ground. Tag line operators positioned outside the exclusion zone, in clear sight of the signal person.

Section 4 — Standard Lift Procedure
  1. ☐ Pre-shift crane inspection completed and signed off.
  2. ☐ Pre-shift rigging inspection — slings, shackles, hooks, tag lines — completed.
  3. ☐ Load chart referenced; Lift Director confirms pick weight is within capacity at the lift radius (apply de-rating for outrigger config, on-rubber if not outrigged, side-load, etc.).
  4. ☐ Ground conditions confirmed adequate (firm, level, blocked under outriggers; mat / pad if soft).
  5. ☐ Swing path surveyed — overhead obstructions, energized lines, adjacent structures.
  6. ☐ Energized power line clearance verified per §1926.1408 Table A; spotter assigned if within trigger distance.
  7. ☐ Exclusion zone established below the load and along the swing path; signage / barriers as needed.
  8. ☐ Tag lines attached (minimum two for loads > 2,000 lb).
  9. ☐ Communication channel confirmed (radio frequency + verbal handshake with operator).
  10. ☐ Lift Director's GO command before lift.
  11. ☐ Initial lift — load raised just off support, paused, all rigging verified visually + audibly (no creak / pop).
  12. ☐ Slow swing to placement point.
  13. ☐ Connectors at receiving position with PPE + fall protection compliant.
  14. ☐ Load placed; minimum two bolts installed wrench-tight per §1926.756(a)(1) before load release.
  15. ☐ Load released from rigging; tag lines retrieved after bolts are in.
  16. ☐ Operator returns block to neutral / safe position.

5.2 — Required Critical-Lift Steps
  1. Engineering review. Critical Lift Calculator XLSX completed; calculator outputs reviewed by Lift Director + a qualified person (in-house engineer or crane vendor lead).
  2. EOR notification (when over occupied structure, atypical rigging, or where the prime contract specifies).
  3. Pre-lift huddle. Every participant in the lift attends: operator, signal person, rigger, tag-line handlers, connectors, Lift Director, safety officer, GC rep. Walk the procedure, confirm communication, confirm exclusion zone.
  4. Written Critical Lift Permit. Section 11 form completed and signed. Posted at the crane during the lift.
  5. Weather window. Wind sustained ≤ 20 mph, gust ≤ 30 mph (more conservative than routine). No precipitation that affects rigging visibility.
  6. Trial lift. For personnel-platform lifts ONLY (1926.1431) — required by regulation; for other critical lifts, an inch-up confirmation is sufficient.
  7. Stop-work authority. Any participant may stop the lift at any moment for any reason; permit is voided and re-issued only after the issue is corrected.
  8. Post-lift review. Within 24 hours, the Lift Director documents any deviation from plan and what was learned, for future critical-lift planning.

Tandem lift failure modes. Most tandem-lift incidents are not crane failures — they are coordination failures. The lead operator initiates a move the assist operator was not expecting; the load shifts; the assist crane gets side-loaded; the assist crane fails or drops the load. The fix: a single Lift Director with override authority on a single radio channel, simultaneous slow movements only, and operators who have either trained together OR rehearsed the lift sequence on the ground before the actual lift. Do not run a tandem lift on field improvisation.
Section 7 — Personnel Platform ("Man Basket") Addendum (29 CFR 1926.1431)
29 CFR 1926.1431(a) — The use of equipment to hoist employees is prohibited except where the employer demonstrates that the erection, use, and dismantling of conventional means of reaching the worksite (e.g., a personnel hoist, ladder, stairway, aerial lift, elevating work platform, or scaffold) would be more hazardous, or is not possible because of structural design or worksite conditions.

Use only when conventional means are more hazardous. If a scissor lift, boom lift, scaffold, or stair-built reach is feasible, that method is required. The personnel-platform option is a last resort.

Outrigger pads, not outrigger mats. Outrigger PADS are the small steel/wood piece directly under the float; outrigger MATS are the larger structural mat (typically crane-vendor supplied or job-built timber crib) that distributes load over a wider area. Soft soils, recent fill, or below-grade structures require mats sized by a qualified person — pads alone are insufficient. The Critical Lift Calculator computes the bearing pressure; the mat size is sized to keep pressure below the soils report capacity with a factor of safety (typically 2.0).
Section 10 — Stop-Work Triggers

Any one of the following triggers an immediate stop. The Lift Director (or any participant with stop-work authority) calls "STOP" on the radio channel. The operator brings the load to a safe position. Resume only after the trigger condition is resolved.

TriggerRequired Response
Wind sustained >25 mph (routine) / >20 mph (critical)Set the load and ground the crane (boom down / parking position).
Wind gust >35 mph (routine) / >30 mph (critical)Set the load and ground the crane.
Lightning within 6 milesPersonnel off the crane; load set; crane grounded. Resume 30 min after last strike.
Heavy rain affecting visibilitySet the load; reassess after rain abates.
Power-line encroachment alarm or visible approachStop motion; back off to clearance; reassess.
Crane functional fault (LMI, anti-two-block, brake, hoist)Set the load on closest stable support; lock out the crane.
Rigging anomaly (audible pop, visible deformation, sling shift)Set the load; remove damaged rigging from service.
Personnel in exclusion zoneHold load; clear zone; resume.
Ground settlement / outrigger sinkingSet the load; reassess ground; re-pad.
Communication failure (radio or signal)Hold load until communication restored.
Operator unable to continue safelySet the load; transition to backup operator; reassess.

Closing reminder. Crane and rigging failures are typically catastrophic. The procedural rigor in this plan exists because the consequence of getting it wrong is fatal. No production schedule justifies compromising the standard lift procedure or the critical lift criteria. The Lift Director's stop-work authority is absolute; any participant may invoke it for any reason without justification. Have a qualified crane consultant or experienced erection-firm principal review this plan as adapted to your specific project before first use.
