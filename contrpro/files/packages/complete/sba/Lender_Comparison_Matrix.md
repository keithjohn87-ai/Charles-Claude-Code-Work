*ContrPro Complete &middot; SBA Suite v1.0 &middot; Updated January 2026*

## How to use this matrix

Pick the lender that fits your loan size, your geography, your time horizon, and your construction-industry needs. The lender choice routinely shifts approval probability by 20–40 percentage points for the same borrower with the same financials. This matrix gives you the data to choose well.

The 15 lenders profiled below are ordered roughly by SBA-7(a) volume and contractor-industry relevance. Profiles include the lender's geographic reach, loan size range, average close time, construction-industry experience level, what they do best, where they're weak, and what to expect from their underwriting.

Below the lender profiles, a separate section covers the SBA 504 CDCs (Certified Development Companies) you'll need if you're financing real estate or major fixed assets through the 504 program.

A decision-tree summary at the end helps you narrow to 2–3 lenders to actually approach.

## Detailed lender profiles — SBA 7(a)

### Live Oak Bank

- **Headquarters:** Wilmington, NC
- **Website:** liveoakbank.com/sba
- **Phone:** 910-200-5854 (small business banking)
- **SBA volume:** Top SBA-7(a) lender by dollar volume for several consecutive years

**Strengths:**
- #1 SBA 7(a) lender by dollar volume
- Dedicated construction-industry team with deep trade-specific underwriting knowledge
- Willing to consider larger 7(a) deals ($1M–$5M sweet spot)
- Strong on dental, veterinary, and other professional-services verticals — but the construction team is a real focus area, not an afterthought
- Excellent post-close servicing relationship

**Weaknesses:**
- Loan minimums effectively start at ~$350K — not interested in sub-$250K deals
- Higher bar on personal credit and time-in-business than some competitors
- Long-distance lender: relationship is by phone/video, not in-person

**Loan terms (typical):**
- Rate: Prime + 2.25% to Prime + 4.75% (regulated cap; check current)
- Term: Up to 25 years on real estate, up to 10 years on working capital and equipment
- Down payment: 10% typical for 7(a); 504 deals require 10% borrower equity

**Ideal contractor borrower:**
- 2+ years in business with clean financials
- Personal credit 680+ FICO
- $1M+ annual revenue
- Loan request $350K+
- Has a clear use of proceeds (equipment, real estate, expansion working capital)

**What their underwriting looks for:**
- DSCR 1.25x minimum
- WIP schedule and bonding letter requested for any contractor deal
- Backlog covering 60%+ of projected first-year revenue (for working-capital loans)
- 30%+ equity in the business

**Application process:**
1. Online pre-qualification (15 minutes)
2. Phone consultation with construction team
3. Submit full document package (allow 40+ hours of contractor prep work)
4. Underwriting (2–3 weeks)
5. SBA submission (5–10 days)
6. Closing (1–2 weeks)

## Construction-specific underwriting nuances by lender type

### Banks with construction-industry teams (Live Oak, Byline, First Home, Huntington's specialty lending unit)

- Read percentage-of-completion accounting correctly
- Understand WIP schedules and bonding capacity
- Will accept owner add-backs for above-market W-2 wages
- Will look at backlog as a real asset, not just a "verbal claim"
- Higher approval rate on construction deals (often 60–75% on submitted applications)

### Banks without construction specialization (Wells Fargo, Chase, KeyBank, PNC at most branches)

- Read tax-return EBITDA literally; underestimate true cash flow
- Don't always understand WIP or bonding letters
- May treat owner W-2 add-backs as suspect
- Lower approval rate on construction deals (often 30–50%)
- **Mitigation:** when applying at one of these, attach a clear "How to read these financials" cover memo explaining construction accounting in plain language

### Non-bank SBA lenders (ReadyCap, Newtek, CLX-marketplace)

- Faster, more digital
- Rely more heavily on quantitative underwriting (credit score, DSCR, time-in-business) than qualitative (industry experience, relationship)
- Less construction-specific judgment but more flexible on borderline credit profiles
- Best when your numbers tell a clean story without needing translation

### CDFIs (Pursuit, LiftFund, Accion, etc.)

- More flexible on credit thresholds
- Slower process
- Often better for first-time SBA borrowers in their geographic and demographic focus

## Decision tree — narrow to 2–3 lenders to actually approach

**Question 1: How big is the loan?**
- Under $150K → SBA Express lenders (Huntington, Celtic, Newtek, ReadyCap), Microloan via Pursuit/LiftFund/Accion
- $150K–$500K → SBA Small Loan via Newtek, ReadyCap, Live Oak, Huntington, First Home (in FL/SE)
- $500K–$2M → Live Oak, Newtek, ReadyCap, Pursuit (NY-NJ-CT), Byline (Midwest), First Home (FL/SE)
- $2M–$5M → Live Oak (sweet spot), Newtek, ReadyCap, regional bank with construction team
- 504 deal → Bank lender + appropriate CDC (TMC, CDC SBF, Capital CDC, WBD, FBDC, Empire State, NCDC by geography)

**Question 2: Where are you?**
- California / west coast → TMC Financing (504), CDC SBF, Live Oak (national)
- Texas / Southwest → Capital CDC (504), First Home (504), Live Oak, Newtek
- Florida / Southeast → First Home, Live Oak, Huntington (FL footprint), FBDC (504)
- Midwest → Byline, Huntington, KeyBank, United Midwest, WBD (504)
- NY / NJ / New England → Pursuit (smaller deals), Live Oak, ReadyCap, Empire State CDC (504)
- Mid-Atlantic / DC → Live Oak, ReadyCap, PNC

**Question 3: How fast do you need to close?**
- Under 45 days → SBA Express via Huntington, Celtic, Newtek, ReadyCap (or skip SBA — see SBA Funding Mastery Guide Section 10 for non-SBA alternatives)
- 45–90 days → Newtek, ReadyCap, Live Oak, First Home
- 90+ days OK → Any of the above, plus banks like Wells Fargo, Chase, KeyBank where existing relationships matter

**Question 4: How clean is your credit profile?**
- 750+ FICO, clean financials, 5+ years in business → Any lender; pick for fit, not approval probability
- 700+ FICO, 2+ years → Most lenders; favor speed (Newtek, ReadyCap, Live Oak)
- 660–700 FICO → Newtek, ReadyCap, ApplePie, Pursuit, United Midwest, CLX marketplace
- Under 660 FICO → Microloan via CDFI (LiftFund, Accion, Pursuit); plan 12–24 months credit work before SBA

## How to actually approach a lender

1. **Check eligibility first** — use the SBA Funding Mastery Guide Section 2 to verify you meet size standards, time-in-business, and credit thresholds before contacting any lender.

2. **Pick 3 lenders that fit** — using the decision tree above, narrow to 3 lenders that match your loan size, geography, speed need, and credit profile.

3. **Pre-qualification call with each** — 30–60 minute conversation to surface any disqualifying issues before you assemble a full application package.

4. **Pick ONE based on responsiveness and construction fluency** — the loan officer who returns your call within 24 hours and asks construction-aware questions ("what's your WIP look like?", "what's your bonding capacity?", "how concentrated is your customer base?") is the right pick. The loan officer who needs to be educated about construction is the wrong pick.

5. **Submit ONE complete application** — assemble the full package per the SBA Funding Mastery Guide Section 3, submit to the chosen lender, do not shop multiple lenders simultaneously with full applications.

6. **Respond fast to follow-up requests** — 24-hour turnaround on any document or clarification request keeps the close timeline on track.
