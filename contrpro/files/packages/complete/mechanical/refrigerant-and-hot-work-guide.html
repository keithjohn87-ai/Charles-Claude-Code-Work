Section 1 — EPA 608 Certification Levels
TypeScopeExamples
Type ISmall appliance (≤5 lb refrigerant) servicingDomestic refrigerator, vending machine
Type IIHigh-pressure appliance servicingMost commercial HVAC, R-410A, R-32, R-454B
Type IIILow-pressure appliance servicingR-123 centrifugal chillers
UniversalAll three above—

Every technician opening a refrigerant circuit needs the appropriate certification. Document cert + expiry on the Refrigerant Log. Disposing of refrigerant without recovery is a Clean Air Act §608 violation — fines per incident plus possible criminal liability.

WARNING — A2L transition. The U.S. transition from R-410A to A2L refrigerants is in progress (AIM Act). New equipment increasingly uses R-32 / R-454B. A2L installation + service requires LFL detection in installation area, leak-tight commissioning, and ignition-source-free brazing environment. Train every refrigeration tech on A2L handling before first A2L install.
Section 3 — Recovery + Recycling + Reclaim
  • Recovery — remove refrigerant from a system into a DOT-rated recovery cylinder using an EPA-certified recovery machine. EPA-required pressure levels:
    • High-pressure ≥15 hp: 10 in Hg vacuum.
    • High-pressure <15 hp: 0 psig.
    • Low-pressure: 25 mm Hg absolute (29 in Hg vacuum).
  • Recycle — clean refrigerant on site using a recycler; suitable for return to same equipment.
  • Reclaim — send to a certified reclamation facility; brings refrigerant to ARI-700 purity for resale.
  • Cylinder labels — yellow top for recovery; never refill virgin cylinders with recovered refrigerant.
  • Disposal — through certified reclaimer; never vent.
  • Log each recovery on the Refrigerant Log: date, equipment, lbs recovered, cylinder ID.
Section 4 — Leak Detection + Repair Requirements
  • EPA Section 608 (Subpart F) requires leak repair on systems with charge ≥50 lb in commercial applications. Annual leak rate triggers:
    • Commercial refrigeration >30% annual leak → repair required.
    • Comfort cooling >10% annual leak → repair required.
  • Leak detection methods: electronic detector (most sensitive), bubble (soap) test, UV dye + lamp.
  • Verify repair via initial verification test (within 30 days) + follow-up (within 30 days).
  • Document each leak event + repair + verification on the Refrigerant Log.
  • Refrigerant management plan required for facilities with multiple systems.
Section 5 — ASHRAE 15 Machinery Room Requirements

Machinery rooms housing refrigeration equipment must meet ASHRAE 15 requirements:

  • Refrigerant detection — sensor + alarm at concentration thresholds (typically 25% of RCL or per local code).
  • Mechanical ventilation — interlocked with detector; minimum cfm per ASHRAE 15 formula.
  • Emergency stop / shut-off — readily accessible at exit door.
  • Egress — minimum two means of egress on opposite walls for rooms above threshold size.
  • Self-contained breathing apparatus (SCBA) — required outside the door for class B refrigerants and A2L in many cases. Verify per local code.
  • Labels — refrigerant type + amount posted at room entrance.
Section 6 — A2L Refrigerant Protocols
  • LFL detection in installation areas (refrigerant-specific sensor).
  • Leak-tight commissioning + pressure test before charging.
  • Charge limits per ASHRAE 15 + IMC 2024 Section 1106 (varies by occupancy + room size).
  • NO ignition sources within the installation envelope during brazing.
  • Brazing on dedicated refrigerant lines OK with proper nitrogen purge + post-braze leak test.
  • Training documentation — every tech touching A2L systems trained + competency-tested.
Section 7 — Brazing Under Nitrogen — Step-by-Step
  1. Verify line is recovered + isolated.
  2. Connect nitrogen regulator + flow meter; set 5-10 SCFH.
  3. Crack open opposite end so nitrogen can flow through.
  4. Apply heat + braze filler. BCuP-2 or BAg alloy as spec'd.
  5. Maintain nitrogen flow during cool-down (slow cooling = scale-free interior).
  6. Pressure test post-braze: nitrogen pressurize per spec (commonly 150-500 psig depending on high/low side); soap-test every joint; hold for spec'd duration.
  7. Vacuum to 500 microns; hold per spec (typically 15 min decay test < 500 microns).
  8. Charge to spec.
  9. Verify subcool / superheat per equipment data plate.
  10. Document each step on the Refrigerant Log.
Practice note — nitrogen purge eliminates copper scale. Without purge, brazing copper in air produces copper oxide scale that flakes off into the refrigerant circuit, plugging expansion valves and damaging compressors within weeks. The 30-second saved by skipping the purge is the most expensive 30 seconds in HVAC.
Section 8 — Hot Work Permit + Fire Watch
  • Hot Work Permit required for brazing, welding, soldering, grinding, cutting.
  • Permit issued daily by GC (or qualified site safety person).
  • Clear 35 ft of combustibles or cover with welding blankets / FR tarps.
  • Sweep up combustible dust + lint within radius.
  • Fire watch present during work + 30-min cool-down (60 min in some jurisdictions).
  • Fire extinguisher (A + B + C) within 10 ft.
  • Smoke-detector + sprinkler isolation coordinated + restored before leaving.
  • Outdoor wind — cease at > 15 mph wind / dust events.
  • NEVER hot work near refrigerant cylinders or open A2L refrigerant systems without LFL clearance.
Section 9 — Hose + Cylinder Handling
  • Hoses rated for the refrigerant + pressure.
  • Self-sealing connectors (low-loss fittings) — avoid venting on disconnect.
  • Cylinders secured upright; never lay-down a recovery cylinder during use.
  • Cylinder weight checks — verify recovery cylinder not over 80% fill (DOT requirement).
  • Outdoor storage — direct sunlight + heat increases pressure; shade + ventilate.
  • DOT marking compliance: USDOT-rated cylinders only; never use disposable cylinders for recovery.
  • Transport — secured + placarded per DOT 49 CFR 173.
Section 10 — Documentation Requirements
  • Per-tech EPA 608 certification on file + on site.
  • Per-event recovery log: date, equipment, lbs in, lbs out, cylinder ID.
  • Leak detection + repair log per Subpart F.
  • Hot Work Permit log + signed permits archived per project.
  • Nitrogen pressure test results + vacuum decay logged per system.
  • Final refrigerant charge documented (equipment data plate + service tag).
  • EPA refrigerant management plan for facilities with multiple systems above threshold.
