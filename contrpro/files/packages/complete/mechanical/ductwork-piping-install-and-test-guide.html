Section 1 — Ductwork: Gauge Selection (SMACNA)

Per SMACNA HVAC Duct Construction Standards. Gauge depends on duct dimension, pressure class (1 in wg, 2 in wg, 4 in wg, 6 in wg, 10 in wg), and seismic + service.

Pressure ClassMax Static (in wg)Typical Application
1 in wg (low)1.0Most return + low-velocity supply
2 in wg2.0Typical commercial supply
4 in wg4.0High-velocity / long-run supply
6 in wg6.0Process / industrial
10 in wg10.0High-volume process
Practice note — gauge selection vs. SMACNA tables. Field substitutions to thinner gauge "to save material" are a common cause of duct deflection, torn joints, and leakage failures. Follow SMACNA tables exactly; reinforcement spacing compensates for thinner gauge but introduces more joints.
Section 2 — Duct Reinforcement + Joint Selection
  • SMACNA tables provide reinforcement type + spacing based on gauge + pressure class.
  • Joints: TDC, TDF, slip-and-drive, S-cleat — match SMACNA T-classification.
  • Seal class — A (all transverse + longitudinal + penetrations), B (transverse + longitudinal), C (transverse only).
  • Sealant — UL 181A (rigid duct) or UL 181B (flex). Approved tape only — duct tape (cotton fabric) NOT approved.

Section 5 — Duct Leakage Testing (SMACNA DCS Chapter 5)
  • Required by IECC 2021 and many spec sections for ducts in unconditioned spaces.
  • Apparatus: calibrated fan, orifice or flow meter, manometer, pressure tap.
  • Isolate the duct section + cap all openings.
  • Pressurize to test pressure (typically equal to design static).
  • Measure leakage at the test pressure.
  • Calculate CL = leakage cfm × 100 / total surface area sq ft.
  • Pass if measured CL ≤ specified CL.
  • Document on the Pressure + Leakage Test Log.

Section 7 — Hydronic Pressure Testing + Flush + Fill
  • Pressure test — typical 1.5 × working pressure or 100 psi minimum. Hold 1 hour for sub-50 psi systems; longer for larger systems per spec. No pressure loss permitted.
  • Flush — full system flush to dislodge debris BEFORE filling. Flushing water bypasses sensitive components (coils, control valves). Flush to clear.
  • Chemical treatment / glycol — fill with treated water or glycol blend per spec. Chemical addition with pot feeder.
  • Air venting — air-vent at every high point during fill + final purge.
  • Cross-connection — RPZ at make-up. Document on plumbing's Backflow Test Log.
  • Closed-loop water analysis — sample + lab analysis at commissioning for glycol concentration + chemical inhibitor levels.
WARNING — never pressure-test with refrigerant. Use nitrogen or dry air for pressure testing. Refrigerant in a leaky system vents to atmosphere — Clean Air Act §608 violation. Nitrogen also keeps moisture out of clean copper lines.
Section 8 — Gas Piping (IFGC 406 / NFPA 54)
  • Black iron (Schedule 40) or CSST (with arc-bonding per CSST listing).
  • Sizing per IFGC Table 402 or NFPA 54.
  • Drip leg + sediment trap at each appliance.
  • Shutoff valve within 6 ft of each appliance.
  • Test pressure: 3 psi minimum for 2-psi systems; 1.5× working pressure higher.
  • Test duration: 10 minutes minimum on small systems; longer for larger.
  • Test medium: air or nitrogen — NEVER fuel gas for testing.
  • Soap-test every joint. Witness by AHJ inspector.
Section 9 — Steam + Condensate
  • Schedule 40 black steel typical. Schedule 80 for high-pressure.
  • Welded or threaded per pressure class. ASME B31.9 power-piping rules apply at higher pressures.
  • Pitch toward drip legs + traps; condensate gravity drainage to receiver.
  • Traps: F&T (float + thermostatic) for steady drainage; thermostatic for radiant.
  • Strainer upstream of each trap.
  • Pressure test: hydrostatic 1.5× working (typically); pneumatic only with engineering sign-off + safety provisions.
  • Insulation per ASHRAE 90.1 + spec.
Section 10 — Acceptance Documentation
  • Pressure test logs on each system (duct, hydronic, gas, steam, refrigerant).
  • Leakage test reports (duct CL calculation).
  • Flush + chemical treatment records (hydronic).
  • Vacuum decay results (refrigerant — to 500 micron decay test).
  • Equipment startup forms by manufacturer's tech.
  • Photos of every penetration / firestop before cover.
  • As-built redlines current with field deviations.
  • Submit per spec — typically pre-final inspection.
