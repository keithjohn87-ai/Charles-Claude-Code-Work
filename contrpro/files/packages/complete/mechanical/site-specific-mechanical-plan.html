Section 5 — Refrigerant Handling (EPA 608) + ASHRAE 15
  • Technician certification — EPA 608 Type II (high-pressure systems) or Type III (low-pressure) required for any refrigerant work. Universal cert covers all. Document each tech's cert + expiry.
  • Recovery — recover refrigerant to EPA standards before opening any system. Use certified recovery machine + DOT-rated cylinders. Log recovery on Refrigerant Log.
  • Leak detection — required after any service work; use electronic detector, soap test, OR refrigerant trace.
  • ASHRAE 15 — refrigerant classification (A1, A2L, A3, etc.) + machinery room ventilation per Standard 15.
  • Refrigerant labeling — equipment labeled with refrigerant type + amount per ASHRAE 15.
  • R-410A + replacements — confirm phase-down schedule + EPA SNAP listing for project HVAC refrigerant.
WARNING — A2L refrigerants are mildly flammable. R-32, R-454B, R-1234yf require LFL detection, leak-tight installation, and proper machinery-room ventilation per ASHRAE 15. Brazing on A2L systems requires nitrogen purge + ignition-source-free environment + post-braze leak test.
Section 6 — Hot Work — Brazing, Welding, Soldering
  • Daily Hot Work Permit from GC. Don't pre-pull.
  • 35-ft combustible clearance + welding blankets / FR tarps on remainder.
  • Fire watch during work + 30-min cooldown (60 min some AHJs).
  • Fire extinguisher (A + B + C) within 10 ft.
  • Verify smoke detector status with GC; restore by end of shift.
  • Brazing under nitrogen — required on refrigerant + medical-gas piping. 5-10 SCFH nitrogen purge during entire braze. Prevents scale inside pipe.
  • Welder qualification — pipe welding per ASME Section IX where applicable. Joint testing per spec.
Section 7 — Confined-Space Entry (Mech Rooms / Plenums)
  • Mechanical rooms + plenum chases often qualify as confined spaces or permit-required confined spaces (PRCS) — limited entry/exit + atmospheric hazards (refrigerant leaks, fuel-gas, combustion products from gas-fired equipment).
  • Atmospheric test before entry: oxygen → flammable → toxic.
  • Continuous ventilation if combustion equipment is operating during the entry.
  • Permit + entry supervisor + attendant + rescue per 1910.146.
  • Retrieval system unless retrieval would increase risk.
Section 8 — Heavy Equipment Lifting + Rigging
  • Rooftop units, AHUs, chillers — typically craned. Coordinate with GC's crane plan + ground-bearing analysis.
  • Critical-lift criteria: load > 75% of crane capacity, lifts over occupied buildings, lifts of expensive / irreplaceable equipment.
  • Rigging — synthetic slings inspected, choker hitches per OSHA 1926.251, tag lines for wind control.
  • Roof access — fall protection per 1926.501.
  • Lift area cleared of personnel + barricaded.
  • Operator certification — crane operators must be certified per 1926.1427.
Section 9 — Working at Heights
  • Fall protection > 6 ft (construction) — 1926.501.
  • Mechanical equipment on rooftops without parapet → fall-arrest tie-off or warning-line system.
  • Aerial lift training + daily inspection.
  • Material lift to roof — coordinate with crane / hoist; don't carry heavy items on ladders.
Section 10 — Hazmat Encountered
  • Asbestos in pipe insulation (pre-1980) — STOP, isolate, abatement vendor.
  • Lead-based paint on legacy ductwork — RRP-certified renovator if pre-1978.
  • Mold in damp coils / drain pans — clean + remediate per IICRC S520 before new work.
  • PCBs in old transformers serving mech equipment — confirm w/ EC before disturbance.
  • Mercury — old fluorescent ballasts on legacy AHUs.
Section 11 — Indoor Air Quality + Construction-Phase IAQ
  • ASHRAE 62.1 ventilation rates — verify at TAB. Increase OA per Table 6.1 if missed at design.
  • Construction-Phase IAQ Management Plan (LEED IEQc3 / SMACNA IAQ Guidelines) — protect installed ductwork during construction; cap open ends; HEPA-vacuum before final.
  • Pre-occupancy flush-out — minimum 14,000 ft³ of OA per ft² of floor area BEFORE occupancy if LEED IEQc3.2.
  • Filter replacement — install permanent MERV filters AFTER flush-out + before turnover.
  • Document IAQ plan compliance for LEED / WELL projects.
Section 12 — Multi-Trade Coordination
  • BIM coordination — Mechanical typically priority above ceiling after structure + gravity (sanitary / storm). Coordinate with sprinkler mains + electrical conduit.
  • Sleeves + roof curbs coordinated with structural + roofing.
  • Power to mech equipment — confirm scope split with EC.
  • Control wiring — confirm scope with BAS sub.
  • Floor sleeves through occupied areas — coordinate with arch / interiors.
Section 13 — Occupied-Building / ICRA / Live-Cutover
  • Hospital ICRA tier 3+ — HEPA negative-pressure containment for any work disturbing existing duct or piping. Coordinate with IP.
  • Live-cutover — written plan + occupant notification + temporary ventilation during shutdown. Coordinate with owner ops.
  • Steam / chilled water cutovers — isolate, drain, lock + tag. Test for zero pressure before opening.
  • Refrigerant leak in occupied area — evacuate + ventilate per ASHRAE 15 machinery room protocols.
Section 14 — TAB + Commissioning Touchpoints
  • Pre-TAB clean filter set installed; final filters per spec.
  • TAB by NEBB-certified or AABC-certified firm. Use the project TAB report template.
  • Sequence-of-Operations review with controls + EOR before integration test.
  • Functional Performance Tests (FPT) per Cx spec.
  • Integrated systems test if multiple subsystems handed over together.
  • Document deficiencies + retest after correction.

Stop-Work Triggers
  • Suspected asbestos / lead / mold disturbed.
  • Refrigerant leak in occupied space.
  • Gas leak detected at any concentration.
  • Confined-space entry without permit.
  • Hot work without permit / fire watch.
  • Crane lift exceeds 75% capacity or weather thresholds exceeded.
  • Code-edition conflict between drawings and AHJ adopted code.
  • Pressure-test failure that cannot be promptly diagnosed.
  • A2L refrigerant work without LFL monitoring.
  • Worker injury exceeding first-aid level.
