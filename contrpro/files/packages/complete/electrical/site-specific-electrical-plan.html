Section 5 — Lockout / Tagout Program (29 CFR 1910.147)

Every job must have a written LOTO program. Every energized circuit a worker touches must be verified de-energized + locked + tagged. Recurring training documented.

5.1 LOTO procedure
  1. Identify all energy sources for the equipment / circuit (utility, generator, UPS, battery, control).
  2. Notify affected persons; coordinate with operations on live-building projects.
  3. Open the disconnect / breaker. Verify the disconnect is in OFF position.
  4. Apply individual lock + tag. Each worker on the job applies HIS OR HER OWN lock.
  5. Test for voltage with a known-good meter. Test the meter on a live source before AND after the verification — confirms the meter itself is working.
  6. Discharge stored energy (capacitors, springs, hydraulic).
  7. Perform work.
  8. On completion: remove personal tools, notify affected persons, remove personal lock + tag, restore energy.
WARNING — never remove another worker's lock. Exceptions per 1910.147(e)(3) require: (a) worker not on site, (b) reasonable effort to contact, (c) verification before removing, (d) inform worker before they resume work. Document every removal-by-supervisor. Many electrocutions involve removed locks.
Practice note — multiple workers on same circuit. Use a multi-lock hasp on the disconnect: every worker adds their own lock; the hasp cannot be removed until the last lock is off. Standard practice for sub-foreman + 2-3 journeymen on a feeder pull.
Section 6 — NFPA 70E: Energized Work + Arc-Flash

De-energize first. Only justify energized work per NFPA 70E 110.4 — additional hazards of de-energization, infeasibility of de-energizing (life-safety circuits, troubleshooting), or operations <50 V where no arc-flash hazard exists.

6.1 Energized Work Permit (EWP)

Required for any energized work over 50 V. Permit must document:

  • Work to be performed + justification why de-energization is infeasible.
  • Shock-protection boundary + arc-flash boundary based on engineered study OR NFPA 70E Table 130.5(C).
  • PPE required + verified on site (arc-rated clothing, gloves with leather protectors, face shield, etc.).
  • Job briefing performed; tools verified; safe work practices reviewed.
  • Worker qualified per 1910.332 + 70E 110.6.
  • Authorized signatures: qualified employee + supervisor + safety officer.
6.2 Arc-Flash Boundary + PPE Categories

Project-specific arc-flash study (engineered) controls when available. Without a study, NFPA 70E Table 130.7(C)(15) defaults apply.

WARNING — incident energy >40 cal/cm² is "EXCLUSION ZONE." NFPA 70E 130.7(A) advises NO live work above 40 cal/cm² without specifically engineered controls. Arc blast (pressure) becomes equally lethal — PPE alone is insufficient. Treat 40+ as a stop-work threshold requiring engineered remote racking / circuit isolation.
6.3 Field labeling — NEC 110.16
  • Switchboards, panelboards, industrial control panels, meter socket enclosures, and motor control centers in non-dwelling occupancies require warning labels.
  • NEC 110.16(B) requires service equipment ≥1200 A in non-dwelling occupancies to also list nominal voltage, available fault current, clearing time of OCPD, date of label.
  • Engineered arc-flash study labels add incident energy + arc-flash boundary + working distance + PPE category.
Section 7 — Temporary Power & GFCI
  • All 15A and 20A 125V receptacles used by construction personnel must be GFCI protected (1926.404(b)(1)(ii)).
  • Alternative: documented Assured Equipment Grounding Conductor Program.
  • Temporary power panels — proper service rating, grounding electrode system, weather- tight if outdoors, lockable disconnect.
  • Cords — extra-hard usage (S, SE, SO, ST, STO, SOO), no splices except molded plug-end replacement, no Romex through doorways or as building wiring.
  • Daily inspection of cords + GFCI test pre-shift.
Section 8 — Working at Heights
  • Fall protection at heights > 6 ft (construction) per OSHA 1926.501.
  • Aerial lift / scissor lift training current; daily inspection of fall-arrest anchor.
  • Ladder selection: extension ladder reaches 3 ft above landing; A-frame fully open + locked.
  • Working on energized circuits at heights → consider arc-flash PPE + 4-point harness + tool tethering.
  • Hot work in lifts — fire watch from ground if welding/grinding sparks could fall on combustibles.
Section 9 — Trenching & Underground Raceway (Subpart P)
  • Call 811 minimum 2 business days before digging.
  • Designate Competent Person per 1926.651(k) trained in soil classification + protective systems.
  • Soil classification: Type A/B/C per 1926 App A.
  • Trench >4 ft requires ladder/step within 25 ft.
  • Trench >5 ft requires protective system (slope/shore/shield) unless solid rock.
  • Spoil pile ≥2 ft from edge.
  • NEC depth: 600V or less direct burial — 24" cover (NEC Table 300.5); other depths for conduit + duct-bank concrete encasement.
  • Maintain 12" separation from other utilities or per local code.
Section 10 — Hazmat Encountered
  • Asbestos — pre-1980 buildings may have ACM in conduit fittings, fire-stop, cement-board. Survey before disturbance.
  • Lead-based coatings — pre-1978 buildings may have lead paint on conduit, boxes, or wall surfaces. RRP-certified renovator required.
  • PCBs — old fluorescent ballasts (pre-1979) + transformers may contain PCBs. Disposal per 40 CFR 761. Coordinate hazwaste vendor; document chain-of-custody.
  • Mercury — old fluorescent tubes contain mercury. Break-and-bag is OSHA violation; use crushers with vapor control or universal-waste recycler.
Section 11 — Multi-Trade Coordination
  • Coordination drawings overlay EC against M / FP / Plumb / S — clashes resolved before installation.
  • Above-ceiling priority sequence — confirm at GC coordination meeting.
  • Sleeves + slab penetrations coordinated with concrete.
  • Lighting layout vs. ductwork / sprinkler heads — confirmed via reflected ceiling plan overlay.
  • Power for plumbing/mechanical equipment — confirm final-connection scope split.
Section 12 — Occupied-Building / Live-Building Considerations
  • Live-cutover plan — define every step from de-energization through re-energization, including circuit-by-circuit timing. Coordinate with owner ops + life-safety vendor.
  • Occupant notification — 48-hour minimum for planned outages; off-hours work where feasible.
  • Tie-in to existing — verify panel directory current; door labels match installed circuits.
  • ICRA (hospitals) — coordinate with infection-control preventionist; HEPA + containment per ICRA tier.
  • UPS / emergency-system handover — never interrupt without owner's written authorization.
Section 13 — Acceptance Testing & Commissioning
  • Megger insulation test — feeders + branch conductors ≥600V; record results; replace any conductor below the standard threshold.
  • Ground resistance test — 3-point fall-of-potential per IEEE 81. Target ≤25 Ω (NEC 250.53 single-rod) or ≤5 Ω where required by spec.
  • Panel + breaker exercise — open + close every breaker; verify trip on a known overload (where lab-test possible) or per NETA acceptance criteria.
  • Bonding continuity — verify with ohmmeter or ground tester.
  • Arc-flash labeling — install labels per NEC 110.16 + engineered study (if any).
  • Fire-alarm certification — by fire-alarm sub; integration with EC scope verified.
  • Emergency-system 90-minute battery test — per NEC 700.3(C) and NFPA 110.
  • Lighting controls + occupancy sensor — verify per spec sequence of operation.

Section 15 — Stop-Work Triggers
  • Energized circuit encountered without LOTO or EWP.
  • Arc-flash incident energy > 40 cal/cm² without engineered controls.
  • Suspected ACM, lead-based paint, or PCBs disturbed.
  • Trench cave-in risk or unsafe access.
  • NEC working clearance (110.26) violated — equipment placement requires field reroute.
  • Code-edition conflict between drawings and AHJ adopted code.
  • GFCI tripping on temp power and not resetting (ground fault present).
  • Insulation test failure on a circuit that hasn't been remediated.
  • Worker injury exceeding first-aid level.
