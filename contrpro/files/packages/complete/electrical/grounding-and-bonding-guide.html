Section 1 — Grounding vs Bonding
  • Grounding — connection to earth via grounding electrode system (rods, plates, building steel, water pipe, etc.).
  • Bonding — connection of metallic components to provide a low-impedance fault-current path back to the source.
  • Grounding and bonding are TWO DIFFERENT FUNCTIONS that must both be performed properly. Grounding establishes earth reference; bonding ensures fault clearing.
Section 2 — Grounding Electrode System (NEC 250.50-66)

NEC 250.50 requires ALL of the following present at the building to be bonded together into the GES:

  • Metal underground water pipe in direct contact with earth ≥10 ft (250.52(A)(1)).
  • Metal in-ground support structure (concrete-encased rebar / Ufer) — 250.52(A)(3).
  • Concrete-encased electrode (Ufer) — minimum 20 ft of #4 AWG bare copper OR 1/2" rebar in foundation footing, in direct contact with earth.
  • Ground ring — at least 20 ft of #2 AWG bare copper buried at least 30" deep.
  • Rod / pipe electrode — 5/8" × 8 ft min, driven full length, 6" max above ground.
  • Plate electrode — at least 2 sq ft surface area.
  • Other listed local metal underground systems.
WARNING — single rod requires verification. NEC 250.53(A)(2) — if a single rod is the only electrode AND ground resistance is >25Ω, add a second electrode (≥6 ft apart). Most jurisdictions require ground-resistance test results to confirm compliance. Don't skip the test.
Section 3 — Equipment Grounding Conductor (EGC)

NEC 250.118 lists acceptable EGCs:

  • Copper / aluminum conductor in conduit.
  • RMC, IMC, EMT (where listed as EGC).
  • MC cable (with metal sheath listed as EGC) or AC cable.
  • Type AC armored cable (with proper bonding strip).
  • Flex / liquid-tight only with length limits + separate EGC if longer than spec.

NEC 250.122 sizes the EGC based on circuit ampacity. Larger feeder → larger EGC.

Section 4 — Bonding
  • Service bonding — main bonding jumper connects grounded conductor (neutral) to grounding electrode + equipment grounding bus AT THE SERVICE only. NEC 250.24.
  • Separately derived systems — generators, transformers — system bonding jumper at the source side. NEC 250.30.
  • Metal water piping — bonded per NEC 250.104(A): water pipe within 5 ft of entry must be bonded; sizing per 250.66.
  • Metal gas piping — bonded per NEC 250.104(B). CSST manufacturer may require additional bonding for lightning protection — verify per CSST listing.
  • Metal building structure — bonded per NEC 250.104(C) for buildings supplied by feeder or branch circuit.
  • Pools + spas — equipotential bonding per Article 680.

Section 6 — Ground Resistance Testing (IEEE 81)
  • 3-point fall-of-potential — gold standard for new installations. Drive auxiliary electrodes; measure resistance at 62% spacing.
  • Clamp-on — non-invasive but only valid where multiple ground paths form a loop. Useful for follow-up but not the initial acceptance.
  • Soil resistivity — Wenner 4-point method when designing the GES for critical loads (data centers, hospitals).
  • NEC 250.53(A)(2) single-rod threshold: ≤25Ω OR drive a second electrode.
  • Critical installations may spec ≤5Ω or ≤3Ω — verify spec.
  • Document on Ground Resistance Test Log XLSX.
Practice note — seasonal variation. Ground resistance varies with soil moisture. A 5Ω reading in spring can become 25Ω in summer. For critical installations, document the test in worst-case (dry) conditions if possible OR derate by a factor per IEEE 142 guidance.
Section 7 — Connection Methods
  • Acorn clamp — for ground rod to bare copper. Listed for direct burial (250.70). One ground rod connection per clamp typically.
  • Compression connector — for cable-to-rebar / cable-to-cable. Listed for application.
  • Exothermic weld (Cad-weld) — permanent connection. Required by many specs for grounding electrode connections in critical installations.
  • Bus connections — proper torque to mfr specs. Anti-oxidant compound on aluminum connections.
  • All connections accessible per 250.68 except direct-buried, encased, or otherwise permitted.
Section 8 — Special Locations
  • Healthcare (Article 517) — patient-care areas have additional bonding requirements. Operating rooms + critical-care areas require redundant equipment grounding.
  • Pools + Spas (Article 680) — equipotential bonding grid bonds all metallic components within 5 ft horizontally + 12 ft vertically of pool perimeter.
  • Solar PV (Article 690) — DC + AC equipment grounding; metal frames bonded; specific listed grounding hardware.
  • Generators (Article 445) — bonding of generator frame; system bonding jumper location depends on whether separately derived.
  • Hazardous locations (Articles 500-516) — explosion-proof bonding fittings; no flex bonded through bushings only.
Section 9 — Common Errors
  1. Single ground rod with no resistance test (NEC 250.53 violation).
  2. Main bonding jumper omitted at service.
  3. Separately-derived system bonded at downstream panel instead of at the source.
  4. Water pipe bonding tap > 5 ft from entry.
  5. EGC undersized for branch / feeder OCPD (Table 250.122 missed).
  6. Aluminum EGC at termination without anti-oxidant + torque verification.
  7. Flex used as EGC beyond length / size allowance (250.118).
  8. Metal water pipe used as sole grounding electrode (NEC 250.52 requires additional electrode).
  9. Concrete-encased electrode (Ufer) omitted on new construction with rebar foundation.
  10. Ground bus connections not torqued per spec.
Section 10 — Test + Acceptance Procedures
  • Visual inspection — every connection accessible, properly torqued, oxidation-free, UL-listed components.
  • Continuity test — every EGC + GEC to its termination.
  • 3-point fall-of-potential ground resistance test — record values; submit to AHJ.
  • Compare against spec / NEC 250.53.
  • Document on Ground Resistance Test Log XLSX with date, tester, equipment serial, and result.
  • Photograph each grounding electrode + connection before backfill / encasement.
