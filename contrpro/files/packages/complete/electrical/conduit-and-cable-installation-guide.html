Section 2 — Conduit Fill (NEC Chapter 9)

NEC Chapter 9 Table 1 — maximum fill:

  • 1 conductor: 53% of conduit cross-section.
  • 2 conductors: 31%.
  • 3 or more conductors: 40%.

Use Chapter 9 Tables 4 (raceway area) + 5 (conductor area) to verify. Or use a conduit fill calculator. ALSO verify NEC 310.15(B)(3)(a) ampacity adjustment for >3 CCC in raceway.

Section 4 — Bend Radius + Pull Boxes
  • Max 360° of bend between pull boxes (NEC 358.26 EMT, 344.26 RMC, 352.26 PVC).
  • Minimum bend radius — NEC Chapter 9 Table 2. PVC larger than steel. Example: 1" RGS / IMC = 5-3/4"; 1" PVC = 6"; 2" RGS = 9-1/2".
  • Pull boxes sized per NEC 314.28 — straight pulls ≥8× largest raceway; angle pulls per formula. Pull-box sizing failure is the most common rejection at rough-in inspection.
Section 5 — Conductor Selection
  • Ampacity — NEC Table 310.16. Calibrate for ambient temperature (310.15(B)(1)) and number of CCCs in raceway (310.15(B)(3)(a)).
  • Voltage drop — informational, not mandatory by NEC, but typically targeted at ≤3% on branch + ≤5% combined (NEC Informational Note 215.2). Reference calculation = K × I × L / Cmils (single phase) or 1.732 × K × I × L / Cmils (3-phase), with K = 12.9 (copper) or 21.2 (aluminum) at 75°C.
  • Insulation rating — match the equipment terminal temperature limit (typically 60°C for breakers ≤100A; 75°C for >100A unless specifically marked otherwise).
  • Color code — NEC 200.6 (white = grounded), 250.119 (green = equipment ground), 210.5 + 215.12 (ungrounded conductors identified by system voltage).
Section 6 — Pull Tension + Pulling Compound
  • Maximum pull tension limit — verify manufacturer spec; typical thumb rule = 0.008 × CM (copper single conductor). Exceeded tension damages insulation.
  • Pulling compound (lube) reduces friction by 30-60%. Match compound to insulation type (some lubes attack XLPE).
  • Sidewall pressure limit at bends — typical 500 lb/ft for solid dielectric.
  • Tugger + sheave size — verify cable manufacturer's minimum sheave diameter.
  • Document pull tension on the Pull Log if engineering required.
Section 7 — Splices + Terminations
  • Splices in junction boxes only — NEC 300.15 except where specifically permitted.
  • UL-listed connectors (wire nuts to compression lugs). Sized to conductor + listed for material (copper-only, aluminum-only, or AL/CU).
  • Aluminum splices/terminations — anti-oxidant compound + listed AL/CU connector; NEC 110.14 listed.
  • Compression lugs — use the manufacturer's die + check with go/no-go gauge.
  • Strip length — match the connector spec. Too long = exposed conductor; too short = poor seat.
  • Torque to manufacturer spec — NEC 110.14(D) requires torque-wrench use where specified.
Practice note — aluminum terminations. The most common cause of aluminum-conductor failure is improper torque or lack of anti-oxidant compound. Use a calibrated torque screwdriver / wrench, not a feel-method. AHJs in many jurisdictions now require documented torque values on aluminum terminations.
Section 8 — Voltage-Class Separation
  • Class 1 and Class 2 wiring may NOT share a raceway except per NEC 725.136. Most installations keep them in separate conduits / cables.
  • Power (600V or less) vs. communications (Article 800) — minimum 2 in separation in open spaces; physical separation in shared trays.
  • Fire alarm (Article 760) — power-limited circuits separated from non-power-limited.
  • Verify by raceway labeling (NEC 110.21(B)) + tray section labels.
Section 9 — Underground Raceway (NEC 300.5)
  • Burial depth per Table 300.5:
  • Direct burial 0-600V: 24" (residential branch 12-18" with GFCI / metallic conduit).
  • RGS / IMC: 6" cover.
  • PVC schedule 40 / 80 (non-concrete-encased): 18" cover.
  • PVC under building slab: 18" below slab.
  • Maintain 12" separation from other underground utilities or per local code.
  • Locator tape / tracer wire required for non-metallic raceway per many AHJs.
  • Sweeps + risers — long-radius bends; protect with bollards where exposed to vehicle traffic.
  • Concrete encasement per NEC 300.50 for medium voltage; reduces required cover.
Section 10 — Acceptance Tests (Megger Insulation)
  • Test feeders + branches with insulation resistance tester (megger). Typical commercial spec: 500 VDC for circuits ≤600V; 1000 VDC for medium voltage.
  • NETA acceptance criteria: minimum 100 MΩ at 500 VDC for solid dielectric cable in new installation; many specs require higher.
  • Apply test for 60 seconds; record reading at 60 s. Calculate Polarization Index (10 min ÷ 1 min ratio) for critical feeders — >2 considered acceptable.
  • Disconnect equipment + neutrals before testing to prevent damage to electronics.
  • Document results on the Pull + Insulation Test Log XLSX. Submit to GC + AHJ if required.
  • Any reading below the specified threshold → investigate before energizing; do not "accept" with notation.
