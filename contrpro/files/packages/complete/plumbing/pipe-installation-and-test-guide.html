WARNING — never use PVC on hot water or compressed air. PVC is rated for 73°F continuous service. Use CPVC for hot water (rated 180°F). Compressed-air failure of PVC has caused documented worker injuries from fragmentation.
Section 2 — Joining Methods
MethodMaterialKey Points
Solder (sweat)Copper Lead-free solder only (≤0.25% Pb, SDWA). Clean + flux + heat evenly + apply solder to joint not flame. Hot work permit + fire watch required.
Press fittingCopper, stainless, carbon ProPress, Megapress. Calibrated press tool. Mark insertion depth + visually confirm before pressing. No hot work — eliminates fire-watch + permit.
Solvent cementPVC, CPVC, ABS Primer (purple — required by most AHJs for PVC + CPVC), then cement. Quarter-turn during insertion. Hold per ASTM cure-time table. Verify SDS ventilation.
ThreadedBlack iron, galvanized, brass Thread compound or PTFE tape rated for gas service. NEVER tape PVC threads — use Teflon-paste designed for plastic. Don't over-tighten plastic threads.

WeldedBlack iron, stainless Certified welder (AWS QW-461). Pressure-system welding subject to ASME B31.9 where applicable.
Brazed (silver)Medical-gas copper Nitrogen purge during braze (NFPA 99 5.1.10.5). BCuP or BAg brazing alloy. ASSE 6010 brazer certificate.
No-hub couplingCast iron DWV Stainless shield with neoprene gasket + clamps. Torque per manufacturer (typically 60–80 in-lb). Verify torque on every joint.

Section 4 — Pipe Expansion & Thermal Movement

Hot lines expand. Failure to accommodate movement bows pipes between hangers, breaks fittings, and creates noise complaints in occupied spaces. Use expansion loops, swing joints, or expansion compensators on runs >30 ft of hot water.

Section 5 — Insulation
  • Hot water — IECC 2021 + most state energy codes require insulation on all hot-water lines. Thickness varies by pipe size (typically 1" for 1/2"-1" and 1-1/2" for larger).
  • Cold water — sweating control + freeze prevention in unconditioned spaces. R-2.5 minimum or per local code.
  • Recirculation loops — full insulation including return. Uninsulated recirc lines are an enormous energy waste.
  • Vapor barriers — required on cold lines in humid spaces to prevent condensation drip.
  • Joint sealing — overlap joints + seal with PVC tape or vapor-tight sealant. Open seams defeat the system.
Section 6 — Sleeves & Firestop

Every penetration through a rated wall, floor, or ceiling must be firestopped per a UL-listed assembly that matches the substrate AND the pipe.

  • Sleeve sizing — typically 1" larger than pipe OD for thermal movement. Larger if floating insulation or vibration.
  • Floor sleeves — extend 1" above finished floor in wet areas; coordinated with finished-floor schedule.
  • Firestop products — intumescent sealant (3M, Hilti, STI). NEVER substitute generic foam or caulk — the AHJ will fail the inspection.
  • UL listing — match the system number to the assembly. Photograph each penetration with the system number visible for closeout.
  • Cast-iron through firewall — many UL systems require a metal collar; don't omit.

WARNING — air-test safety. Air is compressible; a failed fitting under air pressure can launch into a worker. Above 5 psi, prefer water. If air is required, set a relief valve 10% above the target pressure, clear the test radius, barricade access, and follow the manufacturer's test-cap pressure rating. ASME B31.9 + NFPA 54 require these precautions.

Section 9 — Disinfection (AWWA C651)

Domestic water systems must be disinfected before service. Most AHJs require a disinfection certificate on file for Certificate of Occupancy.

  • Method 1 — continuous-feed, 50 mg/L (50 ppm), 24-hour hold.
  • Method 2 — high-dose, 200 mg/L, 3-hour hold (rarely used commercially).
  • Flush to ≤1 mg/L residual.
  • Two bacteriological samples ≥24 hours apart — no coliform.
  • Submit lab report + chain-of-custody to AHJ + water purveyor.
Practice note — discharging chlorinated water. Uncontrolled discharge to a storm sewer or surface water is a Clean Water Act violation. Dechlorinate with sodium thiosulfate or vitamin C before discharge, or coordinate the discharge with the water purveyor's policy. Document chain-of-custody.
Section 10 — Common Installation Errors
  1. Tight-grade DWV slopes that result in solids dropout (≥1/4" per ft to 1/2" per ft for >3"; 1/8" per ft minimum <3").
  2. Tape on plastic threads — use paste designed for plastic.
  3. Hot work without permit + fire watch.
  4. RPZ installed without bypass on critical service-entry locations.
  5. Solvent cement applied to wet pipe (rain or condensation) — joint never cures.
  6. PEX run through metal stud without grommet — chafe + leak in 1-3 years.
  7. CPVC under stress at fittings — radius-to-fitting transition cracks the fitting.
  8. Missing trap primer on infrequently-used floor drains — odor + sewer-gas migration.
  9. Combustion air not provided to gas-fired water heater in confined-space closet.
  10. Pressure test with water in winter (freezes in unheated areas).
