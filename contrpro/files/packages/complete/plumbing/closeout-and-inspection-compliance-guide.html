Section 1 — Closeout Timeline (3 Phases)
Phase A — Substantial Completion Prep (T-30 to T-0 days)
  • Walk every system with the foreman. Self-punch before the GC walk.
  • Verify every backflow device has an Initial Test report on file.
  • Verify disinfection report on file with chain-of-custody.
  • Complete all final inspections; clear any open AHJ correction notices.
  • Submit pre-final O&M manual draft for owner review.
  • Verify as-built redlines current at each foreman + uploaded to project shared drive.
Phase B — Substantial-to-Final (T+0 to T+30 days typical)
  • Owner / GC walk + punch list issued.
  • Punch items addressed within the contract window (usually 30 days).
  • Owner training session — fixtures, valves, isolation, water-heater operation, backflow location + test schedule. Sign attendance.
  • Submit final O&M manual with all submittal data + warranty info.
  • Submit final as-builts (paper + PDF) to GC + owner.
  • Apply for Final Completion + final retention pay app.
Phase C — Warranty Period (Year 1 + per contract)
  • Respond to warranty calls per contract response-time requirement.
  • Document each warranty visit; track cumulative count against budget.
  • Month 11 walk — pre-warranty-expiration check with owner.
  • Warranty release at end of period; archive job file.
Section 2 — Final Inspection: What the AHJ Looks For
  • All fixtures secured + functional (flow, drain, no leaks at supplies + drains).
  • Lavatory + sink faucet flow within code limits (typically ≤2.2 gpm at 60 psi for kitchen, ≤0.5 gpm for public lavatories — verify code edition).
  • Water-closet flush volume ≤1.28 gpf (1.6 gpf for older grandfathered installations).
  • Hot-water delivery time — many AHJs check time-to-110°F at farthest fixture.
  • Trap seals + venting — no double-trapping, no S-traps, all vents tied per IPC Ch 9.
  • Cleanouts accessible + capped.
  • Backflow devices with initial test reports posted at the device + on file.
  • Water heater: T&P relief discharge routed correctly (within 6" of floor or to indirect waste), seismic strap, expansion tank where required, combustion air.
  • Gas: shutoff at each appliance, sediment trap, drip leg if required, vent connector secured + supported.
  • Disinfection certificate on file.
  • Cross-connection control specialist's report on file with water purveyor.
Practice note — final-inspection day-of. Send a journeyman 1 hour ahead of the inspector to flush every fixture, exercise every shutoff, and confirm no recent leaks. A simple wet floor near a trap from after-hours cleaning has tanked otherwise-passing inspections.
Section 3 — Punch List Management

Punch items are tracked in the companion Punch List XLSX. Best practice:

  • Self-punch BEFORE the GC walk — find your own deficiencies first.
  • Photograph each punch item with location reference (room + fixture + view).
  • Assign each item to a journeyman with a target completion date.
  • Update daily until cleared.
  • Submit closed-punch certification to GC weekly during the closeout window.
  • Final walk should produce ZERO new items — if it doesn't, the self-punch was incomplete.

Section 5 — O&M Manual Contents

Provide one (1) hard-copy binder + PDF on USB to the owner. Tab dividers per section:

  1. Cover sheet — project name, address, contractor info, document version + date.
  2. Table of contents.
  3. Plumbing system overview — schematic + system description.
  4. Equipment data sheets — water heaters, booster pumps, sewage ejectors, grease interceptors, RPZ / DCV devices.
  5. Approved submittals — every approved submittal in the order of CSI section.
  6. Manufacturer O&M booklets for installed equipment.
  7. Test reports — pressure test log, disinfection certificate, backflow initial tests.
  8. As-built drawings (reduced + full-size pocket).
  9. Warranty certificates — contractor warranty + manufacturer warranties.
  10. Recommended maintenance schedule.
  11. Vendor contact list — service tech, fixture supplier, water purveyor.
  12. Emergency procedures — main shutoff locations, gas shutoff, sewer backup contact.
Section 6 — As-Built Drawings
  • Maintain redlines throughout construction — both rough-in foreman + trim-out foreman.
  • Capture: actual underground routing + depths, valve locations, BFP locations, cleanouts, sleeves, all deviations from contract drawings.
  • Mark survey markers / coordinates where applicable.
  • Final as-builts as PDF + printed reduced + full-size in CAD (DWG/DXF) if specified.
  • Submit per contract — typically 30 days post-substantial completion.

Section 9 — Lien Releases & Retention
  • Progress waivers — conditional / unconditional per payment. Don't sign unconditional waivers until paid in cleared funds.
  • Substantial-completion partial release — typically half of retention at substantial; balance at final.
  • Final release — conditional final waiver upon receipt of retention. Then unconditional final after funds clear.
  • Notice-of-completion — most states require an NOC to start the lien-period clock; track the NOC + your lien deadlines.
  • Vendor + sub release — collect waivers from your own subs + material suppliers before signing your own final.
WARNING — unconditional waivers signed prematurely. Signing an unconditional final waiver before receiving cleared funds eliminates your lien rights. If the GC's check bounces or the owner refuses payment, the waiver still bars your lien. Use CONDITIONAL waivers tied to payment until cash is in your account.
Section 10 — Common Closeout Pitfalls
  1. Missing disinfection certificate — most-common holdup for Certificate of Occupancy.
  2. Backflow initial-test report missing or not submitted to water purveyor.
  3. As-built deviations not captured during rough-in (years-later RFI nightmares).
  4. Warranty registration cards not mailed in.
  5. Unsigned owner training attendance sheet.
  6. Submittal book missing a section (kitchen-equipment plumbing rough often forgotten).
  7. Punch list closed without photo proof — owner rejects without re-trip.
  8. O&M manual missing emergency contacts.
  9. Unconditional waiver signed before payment cleared.
  10. Vendor / sub lien waivers not collected — your final release is blocked.
